# The Burden of the Signal 
## A Testimony of Survival

**Before anyone writes me off as a headline, a rumor, an accusation, or someone else's distorted version of events, hear this first.**

> This is not a plea for sympathy.  
> This is a testimony of survival.  
> This is what it looks like when a person spends a lifetime carrying more than they were ever meant to carry — and somehow keeps getting back up.

---

### The Cost of Carrying

As a child, I learned to carry pain in silence.

I carried my parents' struggles because I thought that was love. I believed love meant sacrificing, absorbing, fixing, protecting, and staying quiet so other people could breathe easier.

- As a preteen, I carried burdens no child should have carried.
- As an adolescent, I carried responsibilities no young person should have been expected to hold.
- As an adult, I kept confusing endurance with obligation.

By every statistic, every prediction, and every generational pattern, my story could have ended in addiction, violence, prison, despair, or death.

**But it did not.**

I endured.  
I persevered.  
I rose.

I built a life from determination, faith, and sheer refusal to quit. I put myself through school. I moved out at eighteen. For two decades, I worked, learned, failed, rebuilt, succeeded, adapted, and kept moving forward no matter how many times life knocked me down.

And through every chapter, I kept showing up for others.

Especially my mother.

Since childhood, I wanted to make her happy. I thought if I loved enough, gave enough, sacrificed enough, maybe I could heal wounds that existed long before I arrived.

That is the innocence of a child: the belief that love alone can save everyone.

I never reduced my parents to their mistakes. I saw human beings carrying wounds they never learned how to heal.

And because I loved them, I carried more than my share.

---

### When Compassion Lacks Discernment

I believed deeply that God calls us to serve one another. To lift each other up. To share what we have. To help people find light when they are lost in darkness.

But somewhere along the way, I forgot something important:

- **Even love needs wisdom.**
- **Even generosity needs boundaries.**
- **Even compassion needs discernment.**

So I poured.

And poured.

And poured.

Into people who were not ready. Into situations that could not be fixed. Into systems that consumed more than they gave.

I kept believing that if I loved harder, helped more, sacrificed more, eventually things would change. I believed that if enough people treated others the way they wanted to be treated, there would be enough goodness for everyone.

I am not perfect. Far from it.

But I tried.

When people disappeared, I showed up. When people needed help, I helped. When people needed money, I gave. When people needed someone to listen, I listened. When people felt invisible, I saw them.

**And slowly, that constant pouring began to hollow me out.**

It cost me peace.  
It cost me health.  
It cost me stability.  
It cost me finances.  
It cost me emotional strength.  
It cost me parts of myself I am still rebuilding.

Yet I kept going.

Even when my business struggled.  
Even when stress began breaking down my body.  
Even when my husband was taken away.  
Even when friendships vanished when I needed them most.  
Even when family could not — or would not — show up in the ways I desperately needed.

This is not about blaming everyone else.

It is about telling the truth:

**I needed support, and it was not there.**

---

### The Collapse and the Spectators

There were moments I genuinely did not know if I would make it.

By the grace of God, I did.

Then came the chapter many people think they understand.

I was told there would be help. I was told I would not have to carry everything alone. I was told we would figure it out together.

**Instead, what was presented as support became another mountain to climb.**

One crisis after another.  
One responsibility after another.  
One impossible expectation after another.

What was supposed to be assistance became around-the-clock emotional, physical, financial, and logistical labor.

While trying to survive my own challenges, I was simultaneously trying to hold together an entire collapsing ecosystem.

And from a distance, people judged.

They questioned.  
They speculated.  
They repeated stories.  
They formed conclusions without ever stepping into the reality they were discussing.

So let me ask something simple:

**If people were truly concerned, where were they?**

Not in conversations.  
Not in gossip.  
Not in assumptions.

In action.

A meal. A ride. A few hours of relief. A phone call. A plan. A genuine effort.

Anything.

Instead, many watched from the sidelines and crowned themselves experts on a situation they never truly entered.

That is not compassion.

That is performance.

When I was finally breaking — when I was terrified, overwhelmed, and calling for emergency help because I genuinely feared for my life — I still felt invisible.

Even then, the focus remained on what I had failed to do rather than what I had already sacrificed.

Somewhere along the way, people forgot I was human too.

A human being who had spent years pouring from a cup everyone assumed would never run dry.

---

### Refusing the Villain Narrative

Then came accusations.

Stories.  
Assumptions.  
Claims so detached from reality that they would be laughable if they were not so destructive.

At one point, someone even felt comfortable wishing death upon me.

Pause there.

Someone felt justified wishing death on another human being based on stories they never verified.

**And somehow I am expected to quietly accept the role of villain?**

No.

Absolutely not.

Before repeating another story about me, ask whether it actually makes sense:

- If I was taking from everyone, why was I the one left broke, exhausted, sick, overwhelmed, and sleeping in cars?
- If I was using people, why was I the one giving away time, labor, money, transportation, care, patience, and energy until there was almost nothing left?
- If I was the problem, why do the measurable outcomes show that I absorbed the greatest losses?

A thinking person should stop there.

Because reality matters.

Truth matters.

There is no cartoon villain here. There are wounded people. There is illness. There is fear. There is exhaustion. There are generations of unresolved pain colliding with one another.

There are people projecting their suffering onto whoever happens to be closest.

**But I refuse to carry blame that does not belong to me.**

I refuse to remain the designated scapegoat for circumstances far bigger than any one person.

I refuse to allow others to rewrite my intentions, my effort, my character, or my heart.

Jesus said:

> Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do.

I understand those words now more deeply than I ever wanted to.

But Jesus also taught accountability. He taught self-examination. He taught truth.

So before pointing fingers, look inward.

Before repeating stories, seek facts.

Before judging, understand.

---

### The Final Boundary

Remember who I am.

Remember who we are.

I am not a stranger.  
I am not a caricature.  
I am not a rumor.

I am family.  
I am human.  
I am someone who survived what many never saw.

And if loving too deeply, giving too much, and sacrificing beyond reason makes me the villain in someone else's story, then let history judge for itself.

Let the record show this:

- I stood when I should have fallen.
- I gave when I had little left.
- I loved when it hurt.
- I endured when quitting would have been easier.

And despite everything, I am still here.

Still breathing.  
Still standing.  
Still healing.  
Still becoming.  
Still believing.  
Still trusting God.

The lies do not define me.  
The accusations do not define me.  
The projections do not define me.  
The rumors do not define me.

**God's purpose does.**

So let this be clear:

> I am done carrying false blame.  
> I am done defending myself against fictional versions of who I am.  
> I am done confusing endurance with obligation.  
> I am done allowing people to mistake collapse for guilt.  
> I am done letting others treat my love as an endless resource while criticizing me when it runs dry.

To those who genuinely care: come with truth, humility, accountability, and action.

To those determined to misunderstand me: I release you.

To those spreading lies: may truth reveal itself.

To my family: enough. Look inward. Wake up.

Choose healing over chaos.  
Choose truth over stories.  
Choose accountability over blame.

As for me, I choose peace.  
I choose healing.  
I choose freedom.  
I choose truth.

**And by the grace of God, no force on earth can stop what He has already begun.**
