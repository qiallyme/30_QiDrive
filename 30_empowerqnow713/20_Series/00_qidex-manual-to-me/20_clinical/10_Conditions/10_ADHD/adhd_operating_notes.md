# ADHD Operating Notes

## Clinical Handling Note

This file is based on imported self-report and descriptive source text. It is useful as provider-facing context but is not, by itself, diagnostic proof.

## Reported Patterns

- time-blindness and task-duration misestimation
- hyperfocus on engaging tasks
- difficulty initiating low-stimulation tasks
- distractibility from external and internal inputs
- working-memory strain and reorientation needs
- forgetfulness that appears tied to executive load, not indifference

## Day-to-Day Impact

The intake material suggests ADHD-related load affects scheduling, communication follow-through, task initiation, and the ability to sustain orderly routines under pressure.

## Support Implications

- externalize tasks into written systems
- use alarms, timers, and visible reminders
- break work into atomic steps
- reduce shame around missed follow-through and restart quickly

## Provider-Facing Summary

Cody reports patterns that may align with ADHD-related executive function difficulties, including time-blindness, hyperfocus, distractibility, working-memory strain, and task initiation barriers. These patterns appear to worsen under stress and may affect communication backlog, scheduling reliability, and the ability to translate intent into action.

- Hyperfocus and time-blindness are commonly discussed ADHD-related experiences, but severity and presentation vary by person. [source needed]
