# Anxiety and Panic Context

## Clinical Handling Note

The source material explicitly references anxiety and correspondence anxiety. It does not provide enough detail to characterize formal panic episodes, so panic-specific content here should be treated as a placeholder for future clarification.

## Reported Anxiety Themes

- excessive worry and overthinking
- sensitivity to uncertainty and misunderstanding
- communication backlog becoming its own stress loop
- shutdown or avoidance when overload is high

## Correspondence Anxiety Pattern

One recurring thread is message paralysis: wanting to reply while feeling unable to start, finish, or tolerate the response task. This appears to create backlog, shame, and more anxiety over time.

## Possible High-Stress Escalation Points

- high-stakes messages
- emotionally complex conversations
- too many channels at once
- existing overload from other obligations

## Provider-Facing Summary

Cody reports anxiety that appears to affect communication, uncertainty tolerance, and the ability to respond under load. Source material also describes correspondence anxiety marked by delayed replies, message paralysis, and worsening stress as backlog increases.

## Information Still Needed

- whether panic attacks occur, and what they look like if they do
- body symptoms during anxiety spikes
- duration and frequency of high-intensity episodes
- current coping tools, medication use, or therapy context
