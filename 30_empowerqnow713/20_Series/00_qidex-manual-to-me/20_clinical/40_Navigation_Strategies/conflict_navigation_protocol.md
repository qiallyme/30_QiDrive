# Strategy: Conflict Navigation Protocol

## Purpose

This strategy is designed to support navigation of high-conflict or high-stakes conversations without triggering shutdown, freeze responses, or escalation. It provides structure and boundaries to prevent overwhelm while maintaining communication and safety.

**Core Goal**: Engage in difficult conversations while protecting nervous system from overload and preventing shutdown responses.

## When To Use It

### Kinds of Situations

- **High-stakes conversations**: Where outcomes matter significantly
- **Accusatory contexts**: When being questioned or accused
- **Legal/regulatory disputes**: Utility companies, creditors, legal matters
- **Relationship conflicts**: Especially with history of gaslighting or abuse
- **Professional disputes**: Business conflicts, partnership issues
- **Any situation where shutdown is likely**: When past experience suggests shutdown response

### Triggers That Require This Strategy

- Accusatory tone detected
- Gaslighting attempts
- Threats (direct or implied)
- Multiple simultaneous demands
- High-stakes evaluation contexts
- History of abuse or exploitation in relationship
- Feeling overwhelmed or foggy

## Steps

### 1. Pre-Conflict Regulation

**Before the conversation**:
- **Identify triggers**: What might cause shutdown-
- **Prepare environment**: Quiet space, low stimulation, support person if needed
- **Set boundaries**: What topics are off-limits- What behaviors are unacceptable-
- **Gather resources**: Documents, notes, written statements if needed
- **Regulate nervous system**: Breathing, grounding, medication if needed
- **Plan exit strategy**: How to end conversation if needed

### 2. How to Frame Conversations

**Opening statement** (if appropriate):
- "I want to have this conversation, and I need some structure to do it effectively."
- "I may need to take breaks or write things down."
- "I need this to be a calm, non-accusatory conversation."

**During conversation**:
- **Written notes**: Take notes to track what's being said
- **Clarify statements**: "Can you clarify what you mean by...-"
- **Repeat back**: "So what I'm hearing is..."
- **Request time**: "I need a moment to process that."
- **Set pace**: "Can we slow down- I'm having trouble tracking."

### 3. Boundaries and Hard Stops

**Hard stops** (end conversation immediately):
- **Threats**: Any threat to safety, housing, relationships
- **Gaslighting**: Being told reality is different than experienced
- **Physical aggression**: Any physical threat or violence
- **Verbal abuse**: Name-calling, personal attacks
- **Shutdown triggered**: If shutdown begins, end conversation
- **Overwhelm**: If too overwhelmed to continue

**Boundaries to set**:
- "I won't continue if you [threaten/yell/gaslight]."
- "I need to take a break if I start to shut down."
- "I need this conversation in writing if it's too overwhelming."
- "I need a support person present for this conversation."

### 4. Recovery After Conflict

**Immediate recovery**:
- **Remove self from situation**: Physical space, if possible
- **Grounding**: Breathing, sensory grounding, body awareness
- **Support**: Contact support person, therapist, safe person
- **Document**: Write down what happened while fresh
- **Self-care**: Rest, water, medication if needed

**Ongoing recovery**:
- **Process with therapist**: Work through what happened
- **Adjust strategy**: What worked- What didn't-
- **Set new boundaries**: What needs to change-
- **Self-compassion**: This is hard, and you're doing your best

## Do / Do Not

### DO:

- ✅ Set boundaries before conversation starts
- ✅ Take breaks when needed
- ✅ Use written communication when verbal fails
- ✅ Have support person present when possible
- ✅ Document conversations (notes, recordings if legal)
- ✅ End conversation if hard stops are triggered
- ✅ Prioritize safety over "being right"
- ✅ Use "I need" statements rather than accusations

### DO NOT:

- ❌ Continue conversation if shutdown is triggered
- ❌ Engage with gaslighting or threats
- ❌ Try to "prove yourself" when overwhelmed
- ❌ Ignore physical or emotional warning signs
- ❌ Stay in unsafe situations to "be polite"
- ❌ Engage when alone with abuser
- ❌ Accept blame for things that aren't your fault
- ❌ Try to "fix" the other person's behavior

## Success Indicators

### How to Know If Strategy Is Working

- ✅ Can engage in difficult conversations without shutdown
- ✅ Able to set and maintain boundaries
- ✅ Feel safe during conversations
- ✅ Can process and respond effectively
- ✅ Relationships improve (or toxic ones end)
- ✅ Less fear about difficult conversations
- ✅ Better outcomes in disputes

### Warning Signs Strategy Needs Adjustment

- ⚠️ Still experiencing shutdowns
- ⚠️ Boundaries not being respected
- ⚠️ Feeling unsafe during conversations
- ⚠️ Unable to process or respond
- ⚠️ Relationships getting worse
- ⚠️ Increased fear about conversations
- ⚠️ Poor outcomes in disputes

## Environmental Requirements

### What Supports This Strategy

- **Quiet space**: Low stimulation environment
- **Support person**: Advocate or witness present
- **Written options**: Ability to communicate in writing
- **Time flexibility**: No pressure to respond immediately
- **Structure**: Clear agenda and boundaries
- **Safety**: Physical and emotional safety ensured

## Personal Experience

### How This Strategy Has Been Used

- **Utility disputes**: Written communication, documentation, ADA requests
- **Legal contexts**: Support person present, written statements
- **Relationship conflicts**: Boundaries set, breaks taken, written follow-up
- **Professional disputes**: Documentation, structured communication

### What Has Been Learned

- Written communication is often more effective than verbal
- Boundaries must be set before conflict, not during
- Support person presence makes huge difference
- Shutdown is protective, not failure
- Safety must come before "being right"
