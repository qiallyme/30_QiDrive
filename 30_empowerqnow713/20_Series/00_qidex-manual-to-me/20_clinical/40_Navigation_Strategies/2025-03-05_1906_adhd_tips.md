Managing ADHD and mental load can be challenging, but implementing effective strategies can help reduce overwhelm and increase productivity.

## Key Strategies for Managing ADHD Mental Load

1. **Offer and Ask for Choices:** Instead of open-ended questions, provide or request specific options to reduce decision fatigue.
2. **Externalize Information:** Use whiteboards, digital tools, and physical reminders to get information out of your head and into the visible world.
3. **Create Visual Cues:** Set up obvious visual reminders in key locations to trigger memory without relying on mental energy.
4. **Reduce Active Tasks:** Focus on fewer tasks at once to prevent mental overload and increase completion rates.
5. **Set Digital Reminders:** Use calendar alerts and digital tools to offload future planning and reduce anxiety about forgetting.
6. **Delegate Entire Areas:** Instead of individual tasks, assign complete responsibility areas to reduce ongoing mental coordination.
7. **Practice Self-Compassion:** Acknowledge that managing ADHD is challenging and treat yourself with understanding when things don't go as planned.
8. **Prioritize Sleep:** Maintain good sleep habits to support emotional regulation and cognitive function.
9. **Maintain Perspective:** Remember that occasional struggles don't define your entire day or capabilities.
10. **Adjust Expectations:** Set realistic standards that account for ADHD challenges rather than trying to meet neurotypical expectations.
