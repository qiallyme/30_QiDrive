# Core Principles for ADHD-Friendly Organization

**Visual Cues:**

- Use emojis in folder names to add visual distinctions.
- Color-code folders (right-click -> "Change color").

**Clear, Concise Names:** Avoid jargon. Use plain language.

**Logical Grouping:** Categorize information into broad, intuitive categories.

**Deep Nesting with Clear Paths:** Ensure each folder level has a clear purpose.

**Regular Maintenance:** Schedule time to review and declutter.

**Search Reliance (Don't Fight it):** Remember Google Drive's search is powerful. Use it!

**Tips for ADHD Brains:**

- **Use Google Drive's Starred Feature:** Star frequently used folders and files for quick access.
- **Create Shortcuts:** For files you access daily, create shortcuts on your desktop.
- **Use Google Drive's Search:** Don't be afraid to search. It's often faster than navigating folders.
- **Regularly Review and Declutter:** Schedule time to delete or archive old files.
- **Consider a Consistent Naming Convention:** Use dates or prefixes to help you quickly identify files.
- **Utilize Google Drive's Offline Access:** If you work in areas with poor internet, download important files for offline use.
- **Use Google Drive for Desktop:** This will allow your google drive to function like a folder on your computer.
