# Mechanism Description
Dissociation occurs when the brain becomes overwhelmed by simultaneous threat cues with no safe escape route. This is a **shutdown protocol**, not a choice, and is common among trauma survivors and neurodivergent individuals with high emotional sensitivity (INFJ, empathic profiles, ADHD hyperreactivity).

# Internal Process

- Sympathetic system overload
- Cognitive override fails
- Identity fragmentation (“robot speech”)
- Non-volitional motor actions
- Blackouts or “losing time”
- Fight/flight blocked → freeze/fawn

# Why It Happens in This Pattern

Q was:
- Trapped physically
- Trapped socially
- Trapped emotionally
- Trapped by surveillance threats
- Trapped by external judgement (“you let him in, so it’s your fault”)

Dissociation becomes the only available exit door when every other exit is blocked.

# Behavioral Presentation

- Monotone or robotic speech
- Blank affect
- Reduced agency
- “Waking up” holding objects (knife/hammer) without full memory
- Disorientation
- Inability to make safe decisions
