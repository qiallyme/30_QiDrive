## 7.1 Conditions I Navigate:

- ADHD (executive dysfunction, hyperfocus, forgetfulness)
- PTSD (from past trauma — be mindful of sudden tones or criticism)
- Depression & Anxiety (daily management, not always visible)
- Phonophobia (sensitivity to noise — headphones are survival)
- Sleep Disorders (wake-up routines take planning)

## 7.2 What Helps:

- Clarity, calm tone, nonjudgmental check-ins
- Visual aids, task breakdowns, async options
- Grace for forgetfulness — it’s rarely about carelessness

<aside>
💡

Based on Cody's manual, here's how their conditions shape their interactions and needs:

**ADHD Impact:**

- Experiences executive dysfunction and hyperfocus, which affects their ability to manage tasks and time
- Needs visual aids and task breakdowns to process information effectively
- May forget things, which should be met with understanding rather than judgment

**PTSD Considerations:**

- Particularly sensitive to sudden tones and criticism
- Benefits from calm, nonjudgmental communication approaches

**Additional Sensory & Mental Health Needs:**

- Manages ongoing depression and anxiety that may not be externally apparent
- Has phonophobia requiring headphones as a coping mechanism
- Requires structured wake-up routines due to sleep disorders

**Best Ways to Support:**

- Provide clear communication with a calm tone
- Offer asynchronous options for interaction
- Show understanding about forgetfulness rather than assuming carelessness
