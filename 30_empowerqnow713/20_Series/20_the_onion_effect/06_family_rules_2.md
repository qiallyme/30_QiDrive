# The Onion Effect – Episode 6
## Family Rules: When the People Who Raised You Still Don't See You

Sometimes, the biggest onions aren't on your plate—they're sitting across from you at Thanksgiving dinner.

Family can be the origin of our deepest comforts… and our deepest micro-frictions. The way they say your name wrong on purpose. The way they roll their eyes when you remind them you don't eat certain things. The way they still treat you like the version of you they remember instead of the one you actually are.

In my family, I've learned to pick my battles. I've learned how to laugh it off when they tell me I'm "too sensitive," how to silently sit through jokes that poke fun at my identity, my boundaries, or my way of doing life. I've learned how to say "it's fine" even when it's really, really not.

But The Onion Effect lives here too.

Every time I mute myself to keep the peace, I'm swallowing a message: "This space wasn't made with you in mind."

And when you grow up hearing that message in a thousand subtle ways, it doesn't go away just because you're grown now. It lingers. It shapes your tone, your posture, your ability to ask for what you need—even outside the family.

You start shrinking your joy to make others comfortable.

You translate yourself to avoid conflict.

You overcompensate so no one can call you difficult.

And yeah, you show up to the cookout.

But you leave feeling like you've just run an emotional marathon no one saw.

So what happens when you realize that family's love isn't always unconditional—it's traditional? And you don't quite fit the tradition?

You get to decide what legacy you're continuing—and which layers stop with you.

---

**What's Your Family Onion?**

What's the thing you've learned to accept around your family that doesn't really sit right anymore?

Where have you been performing peace at the cost of your own presence?

And what would it feel like to be seen—fully—not just tolerated?
