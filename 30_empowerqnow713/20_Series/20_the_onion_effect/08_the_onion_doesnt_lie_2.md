# The Onion Effect – Episode 8
## The Onion Doesn't Lie: The Truth Beneath All the Layers

If you peel back enough layers, eventually you hit something raw.

And that's where I am now.

After all the micro-frictions, all the dodged meals, all the masked meetings and missed connections—after shrinking, explaining, performing, surviving—I'm face to face with what all these onions were hiding.

And it's this:

I've been molding myself around other people's comfort for so long, I forgot what comfort even feels like when it's my own.

The Onion Effect is not just an idea—it's a map of my life. A timeline etched in small choices. In every time I said "no worries" when I was absolutely worried. In every skipped gathering. In every laugh that covered a boundary. In every "I'm fine" that was actually a quiet surrender.

But here's what I know now:

The onion doesn't lie.

It always reveals something true, if you're willing to go deep enough.

And what I've found isn't bitterness.

It's clarity.

I am not high-maintenance.

I am not sensitive.

I am not too much.

I am simply a person who has always noticed what others ignore.

Who feels the things they wave off.

Who hears the hum underneath the noise.

That is not a flaw. That is a gift.

So if the world wants to keep serving the same default dish and calling it universal—I'll be over here, writing my own recipe. One that doesn't require me to apologize for existing, or eat around things I can't swallow.

**Your Final Onion (For Now)**

What's the core truth you've uncovered beneath all the layers?

And who are you ready to be—when you're no longer carving yourself into something easier to digest?
