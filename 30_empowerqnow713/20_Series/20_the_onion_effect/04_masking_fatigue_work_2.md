# The Onion Effect – Episode 4
## Masking Fatigue at Work: The Professional Price of "Showing Up Right"

There's a version of me that only exists in professional settings.

You've probably seen them—polished, articulate, reliable, a little too eager to stay late, always ready with the smile or the solution.

That version of me is efficient. Palatable. Brand-safe.

And honestly? Exhausted.

Because what you didn't see was the hour I spent decoding how to word that email so I wouldn't sound "aggressive." The social battery I drained just by sitting through a brainstorming session where I couldn't unmask for one second without risking a "performance review." The way I hyper-analyzed every Slack message, every tone, every look, wondering if I was being too much—or not enough.

This is The Onion Effect in a blazer.

Masking at work isn't just about politeness. It's a constant, quiet calculation:

"How do I make myself small enough to be acceptable… but not invisible?"

It's knowing you can never bring your full self to the table, because the table wasn't designed for it. So you contort. You pre-plan. You dampen your tone, over-document your process, rehearse "safe" versions of yourself until your personality has a LinkedIn filter on it 24/7.

And sure, it works—for a while. You get praise. Promotions. Performance bonuses.

But eventually… it breaks you.

Because even if you're succeeding, you're doing it as someone else.

And the cost of that success? Is you.

We call it burnout. But sometimes, it's not about the workload—it's about the mask load.

And the longer you wear it, the harder it is to remember who you were before it.

---

**What's Your Onion at Work?**

What's the part of yourself you leave in the car before you clock in?

What have you had to hide, soften, translate, or deny just to be taken seriously?

And what would your career look like if you didn't have to wear a costume every day?
