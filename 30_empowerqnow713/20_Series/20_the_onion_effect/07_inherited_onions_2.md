# The Onion Effect – Episode 7
## Inherited Onions: The Things We Carry That Were Never Ours to Hold

Some onions aren't picked—they're passed down.

You don't remember choosing them. You just woke up one day and realized you were carrying things that never quite belonged to you. Fears that aren't yours. Habits that don't fit. Expectations that feel like a costume stitched from someone else's survival.

This is The Onion Effect in its generational form.

It's the unspoken rules you inherited—don't speak up, don't rest, don't disappoint.

It's your mom's anxiety living in your nervous system.

It's your dad's silence echoing in your relationships.

It's your grandparents' scarcity mindset quietly controlling your bank account decisions.

And it's heavy.

Because these inherited onions don't show up all at once—they sneak in through patterns.

The way you react before thinking.

The guilt that follows joy.

The instinct to overwork, over-apologize, over-function.

Sometimes, we confuse them for personality.

"I've always been like this," we say.

But what if you've always been trained like this?

This episode isn't about blame—it's about awareness.

Our families were doing the best they could. But survival mode is not a legacy I want to pass forward.

So I'm learning to peel the layers.

To ask, "Is this actually mine?"

And to let go of what isn't.

Because breaking cycles isn't about rebelling. It's about remembering who you were before the patterns took root.

---

**What's an Onion You Inherited?**

What belief, behavior, or burden are you carrying that didn't start with you?

And what would it feel like to set it down—not out of anger, but out of healing?
