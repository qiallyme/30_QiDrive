# Understanding My Neurodivergence & Disabilities: A One-Page Summary

**Purpose:** This document explains the disabilities and conditions I live with, how they affect me, and what I need from a partner. This is not up for debate or negotiation—it's simply how my brain and body work.

---

## My Diagnoses & Conditions

I live with multiple intersecting conditions that affect how I function daily:

- **ADHD** (Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder)
- **PTSD** (Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder)
- **Depression**
- **Anxiety Disorder**
- **Phonophobia** (noise sensitivity)
- **Sleep Disorders**

I am also **neurodivergent** and **transgender**, navigating the world without medical transition.

---

## What This Actually Means

### **ADHD means:**

- **Executive dysfunction** — I struggle with starting tasks, switching tasks, and remembering things
- **Hyperfocus** — I can work intensely on one thing and lose track of time
- **Forgetfulness** — I will forget things. It's not carelessness or disrespect
- **Need for visual systems** — I function best with dashboards, checklists, and written information
- **Energy bursts** — I work in intense periods followed by recovery time

### **PTSD means:**

- I am **sensitive to tone, criticism, and sudden changes**
- I need **calm, clear, predictable communication**
- **Guilt-tripping and gaslighting are harmful** and will cause me to shut down or withdraw
- I may need **space and time to decompress** when triggered

### **Depression & Anxiety mean:**

- I manage these conditions **daily**, even when they're not visible
- My energy levels fluctuate
- I may need **reassurance without pressure**

### **Phonophobia means:**

- **Noise physically hurts me**
- Headphones are not optional—they are a survival tool
- I need quiet or controlled sound environments

### **Sleep Disorders mean:**

- My day **typically starts after noon**
- I cannot "just wake up earlier"—my sleep cycle does not work that way
- I need flexibility around sleep and wake times

---

## How I Communicate

- **Silence = processing or protecting my peace**, not rejection or anger
- I need **time to process information** before responding
- I prefer **direct, explicit communication**—don't make me guess or read between the lines
- I speak in **systems, metaphors, and big-picture thinking**
- **Verbal-only communication often doesn't stick**—I need things written down or visualized

---

## What I Need From a Partner

✅ **Patience** when I forget things or need time to respond

✅ **Calm, nonjudgmental tone** even during disagreements

✅ **Visual aids and written communication** for important things

✅ **Understanding** that my work schedule and energy don't follow a 9-5 pattern

✅ **Space to decompress** when I'm overwhelmed

✅ **Respect for my boundaries** around noise, sleep, and processing time

✅ **No guilt-tripping, gaslighting, or "just try harder" pressure**

---

## When I'm Overwhelmed

**Signs I'm struggling:**

- Silence, withdrawal, confusion
- Delayed responses
- Increased forgetfulness
- Emotional disconnection

**What helps:**

- "Want to pause and reset-"
- Giving me space without making me feel guilty about it
- Checking in gently without pressure

**What doesn't help:**

- Pressure to "just do it"
- Surprises or sudden emotional conversations
- Criticism or judgment

---

## The Bottom Line

**I am not broken. I am not being difficult on purpose. I am not going to change into a neurotypical person.**

These are real disabilities that require real accommodations. If you cannot accept this or provide the support I need, that is your choice—but I will not continue to defend my existence or apologize for how my brain works.

**I deserve a partner who understands that "different" is not "less than."**

This is who I am. Take it or leave it.
