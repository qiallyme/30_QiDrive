2024 Tax Filing Season Announcement

2024 Tax Filing Season Set for January 29

IRS Continues to Make Improvements to Help Taxpayers

Date: January 8, 2024

The Internal Revenue Service has officially announced the start of the 2024 tax season on January 29. The IRS will begin accepting and processing 2023 tax returns, expecting more than 128.7 million individual submissions by the April 15 deadline.

Tax preparation can begin earlier using software companies or tax professionals, with IRS Free File available from January 12. The IRS Direct File pilot, offering a new online filing option, is expected to be widely available in mid-March.

The 2024 season reflects IRS's commitment to improving taxpayer services, including expanded in-person service, enhanced toll-free lines, and digital enhancements for easier filing and quicker refunds.

IRS Commissioner Danny Werfel emphasizes the transformation efforts aimed at simplifying the tax filing process and improving IRS operations.

Key Features:

  • Expanded Taxpayer Assistance Centers and extended hours.    
  • Improved 'Where's My Refund?' tool with detailed updates.
  • Enhanced paperless processing and e-filing options.
  • IRS Individual Online Account upgrades.
  • Direct File pilot for eligible taxpayers.

 

For most taxpayers, the filing deadline is April 15, 2024, with extensions for Maine and Massachusetts residents to April 17 due to holidays.

Taxpayers are encouraged to file electronically with direct deposit to avoid delays and get faster refunds.

IRS Free File, opening on January 12, offers free filing for those with an Adjusted Gross Income of $79,000 or less.

Important dates include the opening of IRS Free File (January 12), fourth quarter estimated tax payment due date (January 16), and various other key milestones leading up to the October 15 extension filing deadline.
