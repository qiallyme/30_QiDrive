# Cody — Interview preparation

## 60-second introduction

I’m a systems-first operator with more than 15 years across HR, IT, compliance, consulting, and business operations. My strength is working between business teams and technology: understanding a messy process, defining the requirements and controls, then building or coordinating the workflow, integration, documentation, testing, and rollout. At QiAlly I have focused on people systems, CRM, finance and support workflows, portals, and AI-enabled automation. I’m now looking to bring that breadth into a dedicated business or people-systems team where I can own improvements at greater scale.

## Likely questions

### Tell me about a system you improved.

Answer with: starting problem → affected users → discovery → requirements/process map → build/configuration/integration → UAT → rollout → measurable result.

### How do you gather requirements?

Mention stakeholder interviews, current-state observation, exceptions, data ownership, compliance/security needs, acceptance criteria, prioritization, diagrams, and written sign-off.

### How do you prevent automation from creating risk?

Mention least privilege, approvals, audit trails, test environments, reconciliation, monitoring, failure paths, rollback, change control, and human review for sensitive decisions.

### What is your direct Workday/NetSuite experience?

Be exact. Separate direct configuration from adjacent HRIS/ERP workflow and integration experience. Never imply module expertise that cannot be demonstrated.

### Why leave consulting/founder work?

`I want to apply the breadth I built in consulting to a focused team, own a systems roadmap over time, and see the compounding result of improvements across one organization.`

## Questions to ask them

- Which systems and integrations create the most operational pain today?
- What would a strong first 90 days look like?
- How are requests prioritized between support, maintenance, and strategic projects?
- Who owns data governance and change approval?
- What parts of the role require hands-on configuration versus coordination with engineering or vendors?
- Why is the role open, and what made the last person successful or unsuccessful?

## Interview-day checklist

- [ ] Re-read the posting and company mission.
- [ ] Prepare three relevant STAR stories with numbers.
- [ ] Test camera, audio, and meeting link.
- [ ] Keep résumé, posting, questions, and notes open.
- [ ] Send a specific thank-you within 24 hours.
