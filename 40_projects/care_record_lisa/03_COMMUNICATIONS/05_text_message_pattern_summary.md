# Text Message Pattern Summary
Date prepared: 2026-06-30

## Purpose
Summarize the text-message pattern shown in screenshots and discussed in chat.

## Observed pattern
Lisa’s messages show rapid escalation and topic-switching across multiple issues:
- Cat / Mr. Link
- Police threats / press charges
- Jeep and keys
- Money accusations
- Wheelchair, scooter, breathing meds, pills
- Social workers
- Otis, Frank, Mike
- Lock/access disputes
- Statements that Cody and Zai must leave
- Blame statements and personal attacks

## Specific message themes
### Police / charges
Lisa threatened that the next step would be police and that she would press charges if Cody did not answer about the cat.

### Cat accusation
Lisa first asked whether Mr. Link was okay/alive when Cody left. After Cody answered that he saw/fed him before leaving and had not seen him since, Lisa later claimed she was told Cody took the cat.

### Access confusion
Lisa stated:
- Frank could not get in.
- She could not get in.
- No one took the lock off / Otis could not get in.
- Then later “never mind sorry.”

This suggests access at the residence was chaotic or contested.

### Unsafe people / household control
Lisa referenced Otis as allowed at the residence / on the lease and referenced Frank coming by. Cody reports these people are connected to prior threats or unsafe conduct.

### Care needs
Lisa repeatedly referenced wheelchair/scooter, breathing medicine, and pills, indicating ongoing medical/care vulnerability.

### Blame and escalation
Lisa used blame-heavy statements, including accusations that Cody planned things, ruined her financially/emotionally, did not care, or took/owed property.

## Report-ready framing
The messages may support concern for:
- escalating and contradictory communication
- possible medical/behavioral instability
- unsafe home environment
- coercion/exploitation risk
- need for third-party assessment
- safety risk if Cody returns alone

## Do not overstate
Do not claim the texts prove motive or prove a diagnosis. They show a pattern of escalation, contradictions, threats, care vulnerability, and unsafe access issues.
