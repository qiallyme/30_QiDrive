# Timeline - Escalation and Contradictions

## Scope

This timeline covers the pattern of being relied upon for care, money, transportation, household work, and crisis response while also facing threats, removal pressure, contradictory instructions, and third-party assumptions.

## Core Position

Contradictory demands made stable caregiving impossible. A person cannot safely be treated as the default caregiver and logistics hub while also being told to leave, threatened with police involvement, pressured by third parties, or left uncertain about whether returning home is safe or permitted.

## Events

### Early June 2026

- Records describe escalating home safety concerns, police-threat context, and the Jeep becoming a contested survival asset.
- Around June 7-9, the broader timeline notes caregiving resignation / home safety escalation and concern about being blamed for neglect while the support system remained unstable.

### Indianapolis Period

- Cody's delayed return is documented as logistical immobilization: transportation failures, rental issues, phone/device problems, banking/card constraints, work obligations, and uncertainty about returning safely.
- This period should not be framed as abandonment without accounting for those constraints.

### Mid to Late June 2026

- Records describe pressure around coming home/not coming home, being kicked out, utility transfer demands, lock/access conflict, and unclear lease/housing rights.
- Hotel / evacuation posture became a safety and stability strategy, not a lack-of-care statement.

### Door / Access Events

- The record includes allegations or concerns around not answering the door or restricting access.
- Context includes unannounced visits, hearing limitations from parts of the house, lock/keypad/battery issues, illness/sleep, and people leaving before a reasonable response could occur.
