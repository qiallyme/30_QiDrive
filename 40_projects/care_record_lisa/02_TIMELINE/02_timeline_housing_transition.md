# Timeline - Housing Transition

## Scope

This timeline covers subsidized housing exit, new-house setup, access/locks/utilities, and later hotel/evacuation posture.

## Core Position

The housing transition should not be framed as Cody pressuring Lisa out of subsidized housing. The existing record states Lisa initiated and accelerated the move, while Cody advised caution and later assisted with the physical/logistical burden.

## Events

### Before the move

- The formal record states Lisa wanted to leave the apartment and had concerns about the living environment, neighbors, reports, surveillance feelings, possible eviction pressure, and housing stress.
- Cody's stated position was caution: do not give notice before alternative housing is secured.

### Move / setup period

- Cody assisted with moving, setup, household organization, equipment movement, and stabilization.
- Housing support overlapped with care support, transportation, bills, and medical needs.
- Related work belongs in both housing and caregiving labor records.

### 2026-05-09 household planning

- The household meeting summary shows the new home functioning as a care environment: groceries, bills, oxygen, bed setup, hygiene supplies, commode placement, and avoiding hospital returns were all tied together.
- Bills, utilities, internet, insurance, and budget tracking were discussed as part of household stabilization.

### Mid-Late June 2026

- Records describe uncertainty about rent, utilities, lease/payee structure, and whether Cody could safely remain.
- Lock/security changes and access issues followed escalating instability and hospital/rehab context.
- Hotel / evacuation posture became a practical safety and recovery measure, especially around the holiday lodging window.
