# Sundowning
**Evening Cognitive & Nervous System Depletion**

## Definition (Plain English)
Sundowning is a **predictable daily loss of cognitive, emotional, and regulatory capacity** that occurs in the late afternoon to evening. It is caused by circadian decline, cumulative cognitive fatigue, sensory shifts, and reduced external structure.

This is **not limited to dementia**. It commonly affects individuals with ADHD, anxiety, trauma exposure, prolonged stress, or high daily cognitive demand.

---

## Core Mechanism (What’s Actually Happening)

Sundowning occurs when **top-down brain control fails** due to four converging factors:

### 1. Circadian Drop
- Cortisol and alertness decrease
- Melatonin begins rising
- Executive function weakens

### 2. Cognitive Depletion
- Decision fatigue accumulates
- Masking costs are exhausted
- Emotional regulation bandwidth is gone

### 3. Sensory & Environmental Shift
- Lighting changes (shadows increase)
- Noise patterns change
- Predictability decreases
- Nervous system reads ambiguity as threat

### 4. Loss of Scaffolding
- Fewer people available
- Fewer external cues
- Less structure = more internal load

**Result:** Bottom-up emotional and threat responses override logic and planning.

---

## What Sundowning Is NOT
- Not a character flaw
- Not weakness
- Not lack of discipline
- Not something you can “push through”
- Not a moral or motivational failure

Attempting to override sundowning with willpower **worsens outcomes**.

---

## Common Presentation (Evening Spike Indicators)

If these reliably intensify after ~4–7pm, this is sundowning:

- Racing or looping thoughts
- Emotional amplification (anger, fear, grief)
- Catastrophizing
- Decision paralysis
- Heightened sensitivity to sound, light, tone
- Urgent need to “solve everything now”
- Feeling misunderstood or isolated
- Compulsive planning or explaining

---

## Critical Rule
**Evening is not thinking time.**

High-stakes cognition attempted during sundowning produces:
- Poor decisions
- Emotional spirals
- Interpersonal damage
- False conclusions
- Burnout carryover into the next day

This is biological, not psychological.

---

## Strategic Response: Containment Over Insight

Sundowning is managed by **reducing load**, not increasing understanding.

### What Helps
- Predictable, boring evening routine
- Reduced lighting before sunset
- Fewer decisions after a set cutoff hour
- Warm food, protein, hydration
- Body-based regulation (heat, shower, stretching)
- Written reassurance notes
- Parking unresolved thoughts for morning review

### What Makes It Worse
- Legal strategy
- Trauma review
- Family or emotionally loaded conversations
- Texting/reacting while activated
- “One more task”
- Doom-scrolling
- Explaining or defending yourself

---

## Reframe (Internal Narrative Correction)

Sundowning means:

> “My nervous system protected me all day and has reached its limit.”

Respecting this signal is **strategic self-preservation**, not avoidance.

---

## Operating Principle
**Protect the evening to protect the morning.**

Evening containment preserves:
- Tomorrow’s executive function
- Emotional stability
- Decision quality
- Long-term resilience
