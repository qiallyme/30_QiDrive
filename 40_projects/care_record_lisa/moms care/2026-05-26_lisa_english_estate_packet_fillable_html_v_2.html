00. Document Status

Working Draft / Attorney Review Recommended. This package is a structured self-help estate planning workbook and document draft set. It is not a substitute for legal advice.

Before signing, Lisa should review the package with a licensed Indiana estate planning attorney, especially if she owns real estate, has blended-family concerns, receives public benefits, has minor beneficiaries, owns business interests, has tax exposure, expects family conflict, or wants to avoid probate.

01. Critical Legal Disclaimer

This document package is a self-help educational template and planning workbook. It does not create an attorney-client relationship between Lisa English and Cody Rice-Velasquez, QiAlly, or any related service. It is not legal, tax, financial, medical, or fiduciary advice.

Indiana estate planning laws, probate rules, health care directive rules, power of attorney rules, deed rules, tax rules, and Medicaid/public benefit rules may change. A document can be invalid, ineffective, or contested if it is drafted poorly, signed incorrectly, witnessed incorrectly, notarized incorrectly, conflicts with beneficiary designations, or fails to account for Lisa’s actual assets and family situation.

Plain-English warning: A beautiful document that is signed incorrectly can be useless. Execution matters.

This estate planning portfolio centralizes Lisa English’s core planning documents so her wishes are easier to understand, execute, and defend if she becomes incapacitated or passes away.

  1. Identify who should handle financial, legal, medical, and estate decisions.

22. Attorney Review Checklist

  3. Does Lisa own real estate that should be handled by trust, TOD deed, joint ownership, or another method?

30. Plain-English Summary for Lisa

Lisa, this package is meant to make sure the right people can help you and that your wishes are clear.

  1. Who gets your property?
  2. Who handles your estate after death?
  3. Who handles your money if you cannot?
  4. Who makes medical decisions if you cannot speak?
  5. What medical treatment do you want or do not want near the end of life?
  6. What happens to your home, belongings, photos, accounts, and final arrangements?

The Will handles probate property. It does not automatically control retirement accounts, life insurance, payable-on-death bank accounts, transfer-on-death property, or jointly owned assets. Those must be checked separately.

The power of attorney helps while you are alive. The Will helps after death. The health care directive helps if you cannot speak for yourself. Each document has a different job.

The key move now is to fill in the blanks, review the plan with an Indiana attorney, and sign it correctly. Do not let this sit half-finished. Half-finished estate planning creates the exact mess this package is trying to prevent.
