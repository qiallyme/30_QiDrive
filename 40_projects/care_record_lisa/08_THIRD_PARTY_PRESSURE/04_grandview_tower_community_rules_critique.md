# Critique of "Attachment #3 – Community Rules"

**Grandview Tower Holdings LLC**

**Focus: Guest/Visitor Policy & Family Holiday Visits**

---

## Overall Assessment (Blunt Truth)

This document is **administrative house rules**, not a statute and not self-enforcing. It is only valid **to the extent it is reasonable, clearly defined, consistently enforced, and compliant with Indiana landlord-tenant law and Fair Housing standards**.

Several provisions are:

* **Vague**
* **Incomplete**
* **Overbroad**
* **Routinely abused by property managers**
* **Weak when challenged**, especially around guests

This matters *a lot* for **family visits and holidays**.

---

## Structural Problems With the Document

### 1. Missing or Undefined Terms

* Laundry room closing time is **blank**
* No definition of:

  * "Guest"
  * "Remain"
  * "Consecutive"
  * "Occupant"
* No distinction between:

  * Social visitors
  * Caregiving family
  * Temporary holiday stays
  * Emergency support visits

**Legally, ambiguity is construed against the drafter** (the landlord).

---

### 2. Overreliance on "Material Non-Compliance"

The document repeatedly escalates minor issues to **material non-compliance**, which is **not automatic** under Indiana law.

Material non-compliance requires:

* Substantial lease violation
* Ongoing conduct
* Actual harm or risk
* Opportunity to cure

A guest overstaying by a day ≠ material non-compliance. Full stop.

---

### 3. Discretion Without Guardrails

Phrases like:

* "Owner reserves the right…"
* "Reasonable charge"
* "Consent would not be unreasonably withheld"

These **sound protective**, but legally they **invite scrutiny**. If challenged, management must prove:

* Reasonableness
* Consistency
* Non-discriminatory application

Most cannot.

---

# Focus Section: GUESTS & VISITORS (Holiday + Family Impact)

### What the Rule *Claims* to Say

* Guests allowed up to **3 consecutive days** without approval
* Written consent required beyond 3 days
* Absolute caps:

  * **7 consecutive days**
  * **21 total days per year**
* Violations = "unauthorized occupant" + material non-compliance

---

## Why This Is Weaker Than It Looks

### 1. "Guest" ≠ "Occupant" (Legally)

Courts do **not** treat guests as occupants unless there is evidence of:

* Permanent residence
* Receiving mail
* Keeping belongings
* Paying utilities or rent
* Listing address elsewhere

Holiday family members:

* Visiting
* Sleeping temporarily
* Eating meals
* Leaving after holidays

→ **Not occupants.**

Labeling them as such without proof is overreach.

---

### 2. Family Visits Trigger Fair Housing Sensitivity

Especially when:

* The resident is elderly
* Disabled
* Receives care
* Needs family support

Blanket restrictions on family visits can:

* Violate Fair Housing Act protections
* Be discriminatory if enforced selectively
* Be unlawful if they interfere with caregiving or familial association

**"Consent will not be unreasonably withheld"** is a legal trap for the landlord — not the tenant.

---

### 3. Holiday Context Matters

Courts recognize:

* Holidays = **predictable, temporary family gatherings**
* Thanksgiving / Christmas / funerals / emergencies are **reasonable exceptions**
* Rigid enforcement during holidays looks **punitive**, not protective

A landlord enforcing a strict 3-day rule during Christmas for a mother's children or grandchildren is **inviting a fair-housing complaint**.

---

### 4. The 21-Day Annual Cap Is Practically Unenforceable

To enforce it, management would need:

* Accurate tracking
* Prior notice
* Equal application to all tenants
* Documentation over time

In practice:

* Selective enforcement = discrimination
* Inconsistent enforcement = invalid
* Retroactive enforcement = unlawful

---

### 5. Burden of Proof Is on the Owner

If they issue an "unauthorized occupant" notice, **they must prove**:

* Duration exceeded limits
* No consent was requested or granted
* The person actually *resided* there
* Harm or lease impact occurred

Most property managers cannot meet that burden.

---

## Special Note: Elderly Parents & Family Support

If your mother:

* Is elderly
* Has health limitations
* Needs assistance
* Relies on family for care or emotional support

Then **family visits may qualify as a reasonable accommodation**, even if they exceed arbitrary day limits.

Denying that accommodation = **Fair Housing Act exposure**.

---

## Strategic Bottom Line

**This guest policy is enforceable only if:**

* Applied reasonably
* Applied consistently
* Applied with notice
* Applied without discrimination
* Applied with opportunity to cure

**During holidays and family visits, rigid enforcement is legally risky for the landlord.**

If challenged, this clause becomes a **liability**, not a shield.

---

## Legal Framework References

### Indiana Landlord-Tenant Law

* Material non-compliance requires substantial breach
* Ambiguity in lease terms construed against drafter
* Reasonable notice and opportunity to cure required

### Fair Housing Act (FHA)

* Prohibits discrimination based on familial status
* Requires reasonable accommodations for disabilities
* Protects caregiving relationships and family association

### Case Law Principles

* Temporary guests ≠ permanent occupants
* Holiday visits are reasonable exceptions
* Selective enforcement = discrimination
* Burden of proof on landlord for violations
