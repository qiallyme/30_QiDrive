# Report-Ready Narrative — APS or Welfare Check
Date prepared: 2026-06-30

## Use
This version is neutral and can be used for APS, social services, or a welfare-check request if Cody chooses to report later.

## Narrative
I am concerned about the safety and wellbeing of my mother, Lisa, who is medically vulnerable and has significant care needs involving mobility support, breathing medication, and daily assistance.

I was previously her in-home caregiver and primary family support. I lived in the home and provided daily care/support beyond limited paid caregiver hours. Due to worsening medical and behavioral decline, escalating threats/paranoia, and lack of adequate outside care support, I resigned for my own safety because the situation became unsafe and unmanageable without intervention.

I am concerned that Lisa may be experiencing medication-related altered mental status or behavioral instability. To my understanding, a doctor previously indicated medication may be contributing to psychosis or altered mental status.

I am also concerned about unsafe people around her and the home. In particular, I am concerned about a man named Otis, who to my knowledge has a history of violence, has threatened me/others, has substance-use concerns, and has repeatedly asked my mother for money. I cannot prove motive, but the timing concerns me because around the same period Otis reportedly had housing and money problems, Lisa’s behavior toward me changed sharply and escalated into threats, pressure for me to leave, denial of access, and blame.

Recent text messages from Lisa show escalating and contradictory communication. She threatened police and charges over her cat, shifted between accusations about the cat, Jeep, money, medications, wheelchair/scooter, social workers, and access to the home, and referenced Otis and Frank being involved or having access.

There is also an animal welfare concern. Lisa’s cat, Mr. Link, is reportedly missing. I saw and fed Mr. Link the morning before I was made to leave and lost access to the home. I have not seen him since and I am not in possession of him.

I do not feel safe going to the residence alone due to threats, unsafe people around the home, and escalating accusations. I am requesting that the appropriate agency assess Lisa’s safety, the home environment, whether she has access to needed medication and mobility equipment, and whether she may be experiencing neglect, coercion, exploitation, or unsafe influence.
