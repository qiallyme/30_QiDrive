## Preserved Unique Emphasis

### Indianapolis Period

The Indianapolis period should be read as logistical immobilization, not voluntary neglect. The relevant constraints included transportation failures, vehicle issues, phone/device problems, financial access limitations, work constraints, communication barriers, and uncertainty about safe re-entry to the home.

### Threats, Escalation, and Contradictory Demands

The care arrangement repeatedly shifted between dependence on Cody for care, transportation, money, household management, and crisis response, and threats or demands involving removal, police involvement, third-party intervention, housing instability, or changed financial responsibility.

Those shifting conditions affected Cody's ability to return, remain in the home, answer the door, continue caregiving, earn income, or safely re-enter the home. Hesitation under those conditions should not be mischaracterized as neglect, refusal, or lack of care.
