# Timeline - Hospitalizations

## Scope

This timeline covers hospital, rehab, oxygen, mobility, transfer, and medical-transition issues that affected Lisa's care and Cody's caregiving capacity.

## Core Position

Hospitalization was not an isolated medical event. It disrupted care rhythm, income, transportation, household planning, and Cody's ability to work. The record shows repeated efforts to prevent avoidable hospital returns through oxygen awareness, transfer setup, supply planning, and household operations.

## Events

### March-April 2026

- Care needs were becoming more structured: medication tracking, oxygen awareness, mobility support, and caseworker/Humana coordination.
- Around April, notes describe prednisone tracking, Dupixent/injection context, low oxygen readings, transfer/fall risk, pain and positioning concerns, and hospital/breathing-treatment context.

### 2026-05-09

- Household meeting summary states the main operational goal: keep Lisa home and avoid preventable hospital returns.
- It records oxygen as non-negotiable and identifies dizziness, positioning, morphine, food timing, blood sugar, pain, and oxygen/breathing as issues to track rather than guess.
- It records that commode placement had already been unsafe enough to contribute to emergency/hospital risk.

### 2026-05-21 to 2026-05-26

- The general timeline describes an acute COPD/anxiety/respiratory flare and a shift into high-risk care coordination.
- Comfort Keepers / Humana live-in caregiving, clocking, pay, and missed-punch issues overlap with this period.

### 2026-06-10 to 2026-06-12

- The broader timeline identifies Lisa hospitalization / unpaid caregiving days.
- These dates matter for caregiver pay, care responsibilities, and rebutting any simplistic claim that Cody was merely absent or disengaged.

### 2026-06-20 to 2026-06-22

- The general timeline records MyChart/rehab/mobility discharge planning context, with likely rehab/discharge targeting around June 22.

### 2026-06-28 Transcript Evidence

- The conversation transcript describes:
  - oxygen needing to be on and situated correctly;
  - dizziness after sitting upright from bed/sideways positioning;
  - commode / porta-potty needing to be moved close before urgency;
  - bed reset, wipes, gloves, diaper/underwear testing, and floor cleanup;
  - Lisa stating the household objective was to keep her home and avoid another hospital stay;
  - acknowledgment that hospitalization stops Cody's caregiver-related income and creates a ripple effect.
