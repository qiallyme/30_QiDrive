# Timeline - Indianapolis Period

## Scope

This timeline covers the period when Cody was physically in Indianapolis and faced logistical barriers affecting travel, communication, income generation, and caregiving response.

## Core Position

The Indianapolis period should not be reduced to "Cody did not come back." The available record identifies overlapping constraints: rental vehicle problems, Lyft/Hertz account issues, banking/card limits, phone/device problems, work requirements, illness, and uncertainty about whether returning to the home was safe or permitted.

## Events

### 2026-05-18 to 2026-05-31 - Rental/income dependency begins

- Lyft/Hertz rental began around May 18-19 as an income tool.
- Week-one rental records reportedly did not show LDW, while week two later showed added LDW.
- The rental became part of Cody's ability to earn money, respond, and travel.

### 2026-06-01 to 2026-06-02 - Rental balance / sticker issue

- Messages reportedly showed balance issue, appointment cancellation, and need to visit the rental location for a registration/sticker update.
- This created a loop: Cody needed the rental working to earn, but resolving the rental issue itself required access, time, and money.

### 2026-06-09 to 2026-06-12 - Banking and rental charges

- Old National overdraft/NSF fees are recorded in the general timeline as part of the rental/payment chaos.
- Key fob / battery / lockout incident and related transport cost are part of the reimbursement packet.
- Hertz corrected a rental agreement one minute after issuing an agreement with add-ons, supporting the claim that add-on billing was inconsistent.

### 2026-06-15 - Complaint packet

- The Hertz/Lyft complaint packet reportedly preserved a minimum confirmed reimbursement request of $604.01, plus larger unresolved damages.
- This supports the argument that transportation failure was documented and operationally significant.

### Interaction With Care Responsibilities

- Caregiving communication pressure continued while Cody was dealing with vehicle, phone, banking, and work constraints.
- The relevant question is not whether care concerns existed; they did. The relevant question is whether Cody's delayed return was intentional abandonment. The current record says the delay was materially shaped by logistical immobilization and safety uncertainty.
