### Assistant
> Short answer: **liquid > aerosol** for almost everyone. Aerosol is emergency-only. Liquid is the smarter long-term play.
> 
> Here’s the real breakdown.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 🚫 Aerosol (Fix-a-Flat style) — emergency only
> products{"selections":[["turn0product0","Fix-A-Flat Aerosol Emergency Tire Repair"],["turn0product7","Fix-A-Flat 24oz Tire Inflator"]]}
> 
> **What it is:** pressurized can that injects foam + air at the same time.
> 
> **Pros**
> - Fast and brainless to use on the roadside  
> - Inflates the tire enough to drive immediately  
> - No tools, no compressor needed  
> 
> **Cons (big ones)**
> - Messy inside the tire → tire shops hate it  
> - Temporary only (typically “get to shop” fix)  
> - Can rupture if stored in a hot car long-term citeturn0search1  
> - You still need a real repair soon after  
> 
> Think of aerosol as the **“I’m stranded on the highway at night”** solution.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## ✅ Liquid sealant — what you actually want
> products{"selections":[["turn0product3","Slime Super Duty Tire Sealant"],["turn0product1","Slime ThruCore Tire Sealant"],["turn0product8","Slime Tire Sealant 24 oz"]]}
> 
> **What it is:** liquid you put into the tire through the valve stem.
> 
> **Pros**
> - Prevents flats *before they happen*  
> - Seals punctures up to about ¼ inch (nails/screws) citeturn0search11  
> - Spreads while driving and plugs holes automatically  
> - Not pressurized → safer to store in car citeturn0search1  
> - Can keep you driving for hundreds of miles (temporary but longer) citeturn0search1  
> 
> **Cons**
> - Still not a permanent fix  
> - Tire shop will need to clean it when repairing  
> 
> Think of liquid as the **“I don’t want flats ruining my life”** solution.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## The honest recommendation
> If you’re choosing one:
> 
> **Buy liquid sealant and keep a small compressor in the car.**
> 
> Best combo:
> - Liquid sealant inside tires now (preventative)
> - Portable air compressor in trunk
> - Optional: keep one aerosol can for emergencies
> 
> That setup covers 95% of flat-tire scenarios without drama.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## When aerosol actually makes sense
> Use aerosol if:
> - You don’t want to mess with installing sealant
> - You just want a “break glass in emergency” tool
> - You never deal with slow leaks or nails
> 
> Otherwise it’s the inferior strategy.
>
