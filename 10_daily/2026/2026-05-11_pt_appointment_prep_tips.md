### User
> Give me a short list of what to be ready or do before PT appointments at the house

### Assistant
> Before PT gets there, have this ready:
> 
> 1. **Clear the walking/transfer path**
>    Move cords, rugs, clutter, pets, bags, shoes. No obstacle course nonsense.
> 
> 2. **Have equipment ready**
>    Walker/rollator, oxygen tubing, bedside commode, gait belt if used, non-slip shoes/socks.
> 
> 3. **Do a quick safety/vitals check**
>    Ask: breathing okay? dizzy? nauseated? pain level? O2 if you’re tracking it. If she feels weak/dizzy/nauseated, tell PT before they start.
> 
> 4. **Have meds/timing info handy**
>    Especially pain meds, breathing treatments, nausea meds, oxygen use, recent missed/delayed doses. Don’t guess—say “needs verification” if unsure.
> 
> 5. **Know the top 2 problems to report**
>    Example: “Transfers to commode are hardest,” “knees hurt,” “gets short of breath standing,” “sleep was rough,” “nausea today.”
> 
> 6. **Bathroom/commode area ready**
>    Clean, dry floor. Wipes, trash bag, pull-ups/pads, sanitizer nearby. No slipping hazards.
> 
> 7. **Water and rest spot ready**
>    Chair/bed/recliner area ready for recovery after PT. Fan/oxygen/tissues within reach.
> 
> 8. **Write down what PT says**
>    Exercises, restrictions, transfer instructions, warning signs, next appointment, equipment recommendations.
> 
> 9. **Ask one practical question**
>    “What is the safest way for us to transfer her when you’re not here?”
> 
> 10. **Protect your role**
>    You are not there to perform a circus. You are there to observe, learn safe technique, and document. PT leads the session.
