> WHAT WOULD I PUT FOR MY MOMS BASELINE NOTES IN HER PATIENT ENTRY

### Assistant
> Put **what “normal” looks like for her on an average stable day**.
> 
> That’s the whole point of baseline notes: when something changes, you can tell fast whether it’s actually new or just her usual.
> 
> Use this structure:
> 
> **Baseline Notes**
> - **Mental status:** alert/oriented? forgetful? anxious? sleepy during day?
> - **Communication:** speaks clearly, needs repetition, hard of hearing, can state needs, etc.
> - **Mobility:** bedbound, wheelchair, walker, transfer assist level, can reposition or not
> - **Breathing:** usual oxygen level range, oxygen use, shortness of breath at rest or only with movement, normal cough/wheeze
> - **Pain:** usual pain areas and normal pain level
> - **Eating/drinking:** appetite, fluid intake, swallowing issues
> - **Toileting/continence:** continent/incontinent, bedside commode, catheter, briefs, assistance needed
> - **Sleep:** normal sleep pattern, naps, insomnia, positioning needs
> - **Skin:** fragile skin, bruising, pressure risk areas, chronic rash, edema
> - **ADLs:** what she can do alone vs what needs help
> - **Typical vitals if known:** temp, pulse ox, BP, HR ranges
> - **Known chronic issues:** COPD, chronic knee pain, back pain, allergies, mobility limits, etc.
> - **What counts as a deviation:** what usually means something is off for her
> 
> Here’s a **paste-ready baseline template**:
> 
> **Baseline Notes:**  
> Patient is usually [alert/oriented x__ / mildly forgetful / etc.].  
> Normally communicates [clearly / softly / needs repetition / etc.].  
> Mobility baseline: [bedbound / wheelchair-dependent / walker / needs __ assist for transfers].  
> Respiratory baseline: usual O2 saturation around [__–__]% on [room air / __ L oxygen]. Normally has [no SOB at rest / mild SOB with exertion / chronic cough / wheeze].  
> Pain baseline: chronic pain in [areas] usually around [__]/10.  
> Usual toileting baseline: [continent/incontinent / bedside commode / briefs / catheter], needs help with [__].  
> Eating/drinking baseline: [good/fair/poor] appetite, [any swallowing issues or not].  
> Sleep baseline: usually sleeps [well/poorly], prefers [position], may need [pillows/repositioning].  
> Skin baseline: [intact / fragile / chronic redness / edema in __].  
> ADL baseline: needs help with [bathing, dressing, transfers, meds, toileting, etc.].  
> Known chronic conditions impacting baseline: [list].  
> Signs patient is off baseline include: [more confusion, lower O2, worse pain, less intake, new weakness, etc.].
> 
> A **simple real-world version** would look like:
> 
> **Baseline Notes:**  
> Patient usually alert and able to communicate needs. Wheelchair-dependent and needs assistance with transfers and most ADLs. Chronic back and knee pain at baseline, usually manageable but worsens with poor positioning. Respiratory baseline includes occasional shortness of breath with exertion; usual O2 sat approximately [fill in]% on [fill in oxygen setting or room air]. Sleeps best when properly positioned with torso aligned and pressure relieved. Requires monitoring for pain increase, breathing changes, reduced mobility, poor intake, skin breakdown, or confusion.
> 
> Big rule: **baseline notes are not today’s drama.** They’re the stable reference point.
