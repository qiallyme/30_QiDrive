> For **prednisone/prednisolone**, the usual adult daily range is **5–60 mg per day**, depending on the condition. Higher doses exist, but that is **doctor-directed only**. Mayo Clinic and DailyMed both list typical adult starting doses up to **60 mg/day**. citeturn903196search3turn903196search7
> 
> For breathing flare “burst” dosing, common outpatient regimens are often **40–60 mg/day**, usually **once daily** or split into **2 doses**. citeturn903196search1
> 
> **Time between doses:**
> 
> - **Once daily:** take in the **morning with food**.
> - **Twice daily:** space about **12 hours apart**, but avoid late evening if possible because it can cause insomnia/jitters.
> - **Do not stack extra doses** just because symptoms feel worse.
> 
> The NHS/HSE guidance says it is usually taken **once daily in the morning** to reduce nausea and sleep problems. citeturn903196search11turn903196search18
> 
> Given the vitals you mentioned earlier — **heart rate around 107–113 and glucose 186** — prednisone can make both worse. So the clean rule is:
> 
> **Do not exceed the prescribed daily amount. If no prescription directions are clear, treat 60 mg/day as the “do not cross without clinician approval” line.**
> 
> If breathing is worsening, oxygen is dropping, lips/face look blue/gray, confusion appears, chest pain happens, or she cannot speak in full sentences: **that is not “more prednisone” territory — that is urgent care/ER territory.**
