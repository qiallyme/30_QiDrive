### Assistant
> Yep. For a **ResMed CPAP/BiPAP setup**, the normal manufacturer-style cleaning is usually **warm water + mild soap**, then rinse very well and air dry. ResMed specifically warns against stronger cleaning products because they can damage parts or leave residue. citeturn228216search0
> 
> If her **doctor specifically said peroxide**, they may be thinking of it as an extra disinfecting soak because peroxide can help break down organic residue and reduce germs. Some clinical handouts mention a **50% hydrogen peroxide solution** for tubing/chambers, but that is not ResMed’s everyday default. citeturn228216search7turn228216search8
> 
> **How people usually do it when peroxide is allowed:**
> 
> 1. **Unplug machine.**
> 2. **Remove mask, tubing, and water chamber.**
> 3. **Wash first with mild soap and warm water.** Peroxide is not a magic substitute for physically removing slime/oils. Rude but true.
> 4. **Soak only the removable washable parts** — usually tubing/water chamber/mask parts if approved — in a diluted peroxide mix.
>    - Common handout version: **half 3% hydrogen peroxide + half water**.
> 5. **Rinse extremely well** with clean warm water.
> 6. **Air dry completely** on a clean towel, out of direct sunlight.
> 7. **Do not pour peroxide into the actual machine.** Never run peroxide through the powered device.
> 
> **Important caution:** I would not use peroxide on soft mask cushions, seals, foam, filters, or anything electrical unless the doctor/DME specifically said those parts are okay. Peroxide can degrade rubber/silicone over time, and residue is not something your mom needs to breathe with COPD/breathing vulnerability.
> 
> **Why it matters:** CPAP equipment can collect moisture, skin oils, bacteria, and mold-ish gunk. Cleaning reduces exposure and keeps airflow/seal working. But over-cleaning with harsh stuff can damage the equipment or leave lung-irritating residue — so the rinse/dry step is the whole ballgame.
> 
> For the care log:
> 
> **CPAP/ResMed cleaning note:** Doctor advised peroxide cleaning. Verify exact parts and dilution with DME/doctor/pharmacist. Default safe method remains mild soap + warm water, thorough rinse, full air dry. Do not put peroxide in machine body.
