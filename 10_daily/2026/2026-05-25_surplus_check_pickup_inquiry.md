> 
> **Why they asked for your address**
> - Yes, most likely it’s tied to **sending the surplus funds check**.
> - Courts need a **confirmed mailing address on record** before releasing money.
> - It’s also a liability thing—once they cut the check, they want a clean audit trail showing it went to the correct address.

> - Your filing (address update) has to be:
>   - Received → entered into the system → attached to your case → reviewed (sometimes)
> - Then finance/accounting processes the disbursement.
> - That’s **not instant**, and weekends slow it down.
> 
> **Can you walk in and get the check?**
> - Possible, but low probability unless:
>   - The check is **already issued but not mailed yet**
>   - OR they allow **in-person pickup with ID** (some clerks do, some don’t)
> 
> **What to do tomorrow (tight execution plan)**

> > “I filed an updated mailing address for case [number]. I wanted to check the status of the surplus disbursement and see if the check has been issued yet. If it hasn’t been mailed, is it possible to pick it up in person?”
>

> - **“Not processed yet” →** ask when disbursements are typically issued
> - **“Already mailed” →** confirm the address immediately
> - **“Ready / printed” →** push (politely) for pickup with ID
> 
> **Reality check**
> - Courts move slow. Even if everything is clean, you’re usually looking at **a few days to a couple weeks**, not next-day money.
