> 
> ## Bed Prep Plan for Mom
> 
> ### 1. Immediate safety check
> Before moving her or changing anything:
> 
> - Check breathing: is she breathing comfortably?
> - Check oxygen setup: tubing clear, not kinked, cannula placed correctly.
> - Check mental state: normal for her tonight or unusually confused?
> - Check pain level before transfer.
> - Make sure path to bed/commode is clear.
> 
> **Red flag:** If she is severely short of breath, unusually confused, has chest pain, falls, has new weakness, or oxygen is staying low despite oxygen/treatment, escalate. Call nurse line/doctor, or 911 if severe.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 2. Prep the bed before she gets in
> 
> ### Layer the bed like a caregiver who is tired but not stupid — because future you deserves mercy.
> 
> Use this order:
> 
> 1. Mattress protector / waterproof layer  
> 2. Fitted sheet  
> 3. Disposable or washable bed pad under hips/bottom area  
> 4. Optional second pad across the lower back/hip zone  
> 5. Top sheet or light blanket  
> 6. Main blanket within reach  
> 7. Extra blanket nearby, not buried across the room  
> 
> Best setup: **double-layer if possible**  
> Waterproof layer → sheet → pad.  
> That way if there’s urine/stool/sweat, you are not remaking the whole damn bed at 3 AM.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 3. Positioning setup
> 
> Have ready:
> 
> - Pillows behind back/side if she needs side support
> - Pillow under knees if back/knee pain is bad
> - Pillow or rolled towel to protect shoulder/arm position
> - Keep head elevated if breathing is better that way
> - Make sure she can reach call phone/bell/water without twisting
> 
> For COPD/breathing: avoid laying her completely flat unless she specifically tolerates it well. Slight elevation usually makes more sense.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 4. Bedside station
> 
> Put these within reach:
> 
> - Water
> - Tissues/wipes
> - Trash bag or small trash can
> - Phone/call device
> - Remote
> - Glasses if needed
> - Inhaler/nebulizer supplies only if they are supposed to be accessible
> - Emesis bag/bowl if nausea has been happening
> - Hand sanitizer
> - Flashlight/night light
> - Adult briefs/pads nearby
> - Gloves/wipes for caregiver
> 
> Do **not** leave meds loose at bedside unless that is part of a verified med safety plan. Especially with morphine/opioids, gabapentin, BP meds, steroids, respiratory meds, etc. Those need controlled handling.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 5. Transfer setup
> 
> Before she moves:
> 
> - Lock wheelchair/commode/bedside equipment.
> - Non-slip socks or shoes on.
> - Bed height safe.
> - Clear floor.
> - Oxygen tubing routed so nobody trips.
> - Tell her the steps before doing them.
> 
> Script:
> 
> > “You’re in control. I’m just setting the bed up so this transfer is safe and easy. Let’s do the safest next step first.”
> 
> During transfer:
> 
> - Don’t rush her.
> - One instruction at a time.
> - Let her pause if breathing/pain spikes.
> - If she gets weak, dizzy, or panicky, stop and stabilize.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 6. Bathroom / commode prevention setup
> 
> If overnight bowel/urine issues are possible:
> 
> - Bedside commode close enough but not blocking path
> - Wipes + gloves + trash bag nearby
> - Extra brief/pad ready
> - Clean nightgown/pants nearby
> - Barrier cream if skin irritation risk
> - Laundry bag or plastic bag ready for soiled items
> 
> This is not “extra.” This is preventing chaos.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 7. Comfort closeout
> 
> Once she’s in bed:
> 
> - Confirm pain position: knees, back, shoulder supported
> - Confirm breathing position: head elevated enough
> - Confirm oxygen tubing is clear
> - Confirm she has water and phone/call button
> - Confirm blanket temperature
> - Confirm commode path is clear
> - Ask one final question only:
> 
> > “Are you safe and comfortable enough for me to step away?”
> 
> Not “Do you need anything else?” because that opens the portal.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 8. Document this
> 
> Quick care log:
> 
> **Bed Prep / Night Setup**  
> Date/time:  
> Breathing/O2 status:  
> Pain level/location:  
> Transfer notes:  
> Bed pads/briefs used:  
> Food/water offered:  
> Meds due/given:  
> Concerns:  
> Next check time:
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 9. What can wait
> 
> These can wait unless they’re safety issues:
> 
> - Perfect room organization
> - Full laundry reset
> - Deep cleaning
> - Long emotional conversations
> - Re-explaining the entire care plan
> - Anything that turns bedtime into a family summit
> 
> Tonight’s goal is simple: **safe, dry, breathing okay, pain managed, reachable supplies, low drama.**

> ## Mom Bedtime Prep Checklist

> - Nebulizer meds in stock
> - Respiratory meds/supplies not buried or missing
>

> - Diaper/brief good
> - Wipes reachable
> - Bedside commode ready
> - Trash/bag nearby if needed
> 
> ### 5. Bed + comfort

> - Blankets good
> - Pillows good
> - Temperature comfortable
> - Thermostat checked
> - Windows checked
> 
> ### 6. Room setup
> - Light off
> - Night light on
> - TV functioning
> - Remote reachable
> - Glasses available
> - Phone charger plugged in and reachable
> - Phone within reach
> 
> ### 7. Mobility + emergency setup
> - Wheelchair positioned
> - Wheelchair plugged in/charging if needed
> - Transfer path clear enough
> - Front door emergency-ready
> - Doors checked/secured
> - Oxygen/tubing not creating a trip trap
> 
> ### 8. Vitals rule
> Only take vitals at bedtime if there is a concern, such as:
> 
> - Breathing seems off
> - She seems unusually confused, weak, pale, sweaty, dizzy, or anxious
> - Pain is much worse than usual
> - Oxygen concern
> - Medication concern
> - She says something feels wrong
> 
> Otherwise: don’t turn bedtime into a hospital shift. We are doing **prepared and safe**, not obsessive.
