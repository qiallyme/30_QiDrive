### Assistant
> ## Indiana vehicle title transfer — what you need
> 
> For a normal transfer into your name, bring this to the **Indiana BMV**:
> 
> ### Core documents
> 
> 1. **The current certificate of title**
>    - The current owner/seller must complete the seller section.
>    - Your name/address goes in the purchaser section.
>    - Indiana says the seller and purchaser sections should be completed before you go to the branch. citeturn495276view0
> 
> 2. **Application for Certificate of Title — State Form 205**
>    - This is the main Indiana title application form. citeturn934830view1
> 
> 3. **Odometer disclosure**
>    - Usually on the title itself.
>    - If not completed on the title, use **Odometer Disclosure Statement — State Form 43230**. citeturn934830view1
> 
> 4. **Bill of sale / sale price / gift info**
>    - If it’s a gift, write it clearly as a gift.
>    - If it’s a sale, Indiana private-party sales tax is paid at the branch when you apply for the title. citeturn495276view0
> 
> 5. **Lien release, if there is any lien**
>    - Any lienholder must release their interest before the vehicle can properly transfer. Indiana accepts a lienholder sign-off on the title or a lien-release letter. citeturn934830view2
> 
> 6. **Your ID**
>    - Bring your Indiana license/state ID.
> 
> 7. **Money for fees/taxes**
>    - Title issue/duplicate/replacement is listed as **$15** on the BMV fee chart, with a **$30 late title penalty** if applicable. citeturn578517search1
>    - Indiana says title must be applied for within **45 days** after purchase/acquisition or a penalty is charged. citeturn495276view0
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Important for your situation
> 
> If your mom is the titled owner and she is **willing to sign**, easiest path is:
> 
> **Mom signs seller section → you complete purchaser section → you take title + Form 205 + ID + payment to BMV.**
> 
> If she **cannot go in person**, Indiana has **Limited Power of Attorney — State Form 1940**, but she would still need to grant/sign that authority. citeturn934830view1
> 
> If she **refuses to sign**, BMV will not magically put it in your name just because you paid for it. That becomes a proof/legal issue, not a normal title-transfer issue. Paper trail matters: payment records, written agreement, texts, POA language, etc.
> 
> Also: if there is an **electronic title**, Indiana now allows electronic or paper titles, but removing/transferring ownership can still require printing/signing; if the departing owner cannot sign, BMV says a court order may be required in owner-removal situations. citeturn495276view1
