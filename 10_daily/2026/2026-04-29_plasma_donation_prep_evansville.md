> ### What to do before you go
> 
> **1. Eat a real meal 2–3 hours before.**  
> Not greasy. Do protein + carbs: eggs/toast, chicken/rice, tuna sandwich, peanut butter toast, yogurt + granola, etc. Avoid fried food, fast food, heavy dairy, and super fatty meals because they can mess with the plasma quality and get you deferred.
> 
> **2. Hydrate now.**  
> Drink water steadily. Do not chug a gallon right before you walk in like a chaos goblin. Aim for clear/light yellow pee. CSL specifically says to drink lots of water, avoid caffeine, eat healthy, and skip high-fat foods before donating. citeturn988744search4

> For first-time donors, bring:
> 
> - Valid photo ID  
> - Proof of Social Security, usually Social Security card, W-2, tax form, or paycheck stub showing full SSN depending on center rules  
> - Proof of current address, like current ID address, lease, utility bill, or recent mail  
> 
> Octapharma’s Evansville page says first-time donors need photo ID, Social Security card, and proof of current address such as mail postmarked in the last 30 days or a current lease. citeturn988744search2 CSL lists valid government ID, proof of Social Security, and proof of current residency/current lease. citeturn988744search4

> **4. Wear sleeves that roll above the elbow.**  
> Bring headphones/charger. First visit can take longer because of registration, screening, and physical exam. Grifols says the actual donation is usually about 60 minutes, but first visits take longer due to documents and exam. citeturn988744search0
