> Neutral opinion: **not great right now.**

> Biscuits and gravy + Greek yogurt + milk is a lot of dairy, and if she already has **upset stomach, diarrhea, indigestion, or heartburn**, dairy can absolutely aggravate it—especially milk and creamy/fatty foods like gravy. Lactose intolerance commonly causes diarrhea, gas, bloating, nausea, and abdominal discomfort after dairy, and diarrhea itself can temporarily make people more lactose-sensitive. citeturn406083search0turn406083search5turn406083search7
> 
> The nuance: **Greek yogurt is usually the least suspicious of the three** because yogurt can be easier to tolerate than milk for some people. But stacked with **milk + gravy**, it becomes a dairy pileup. Cleveland Clinic specifically lists **milk and dairy products** among foods to avoid during diarrhea/upset stomach periods. citeturn406083search1turn406083search4
> 
> My objective read:
> 
> **Today’s move:** pause the milk-heavy stuff for 24–48 hours and go bland/simple.
> 
> Better options:
> - toast, crackers, rice, bananas, applesauce
> - broth/soup
> - potatoes
> - small amounts of lean protein
> - water or electrolyte drink
> 
> Avoid for now:
> - milk
> - gravy/cream sauces
> - greasy food
> - spicy food
> - lots of sugar
> - caffeine/carbonation if reflux/diarrhea is active
> 
> Also: if she recently had antibiotics, diarrhea could be from that too, not just dairy. But dairy is an easy variable to remove. No need to make it philosophical. Just run the experiment: **no milk/gravy for a day, see if stomach settles.**
