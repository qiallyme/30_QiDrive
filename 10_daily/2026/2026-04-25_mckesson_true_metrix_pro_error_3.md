> ## What “Error 3” actually means
> **Error 3 = Strip/sample issue.**  
> The meter is not getting a valid reading from the test strip.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Root Causes (ranked by likelihood)
> 1. **Insufficient blood sample**
>    - Most common. Strip didn’t fill completely.
> 
> 2. **Blood applied incorrectly**
>    - Dropped on top instead of touching edge intake channel.
> 
> 3. **Damaged or contaminated strip**
>    - Bent, touched, or exposed to moisture.
> 
> 4. **Strip inserted wrong / not fully seated**
> 
> 5. **Timing issue**
>    - Blood applied too early or too late after strip insertion.
> 
> 6. **Expired strips or bad storage**
>    - Heat, humidity (this matters given what you described about your environment).
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Fast Fix Protocol (do this exactly)
> Run this like a checklist—don’t improvise:
> 
> 1. **Eject strip → discard**
> 2. Wash + fully dry hands (moisture = bad read)
> 3. Insert **new strip fully**
> 4. Wait for **blood drop icon**
> 5. Lance finger → get a **full hanging drop**
> 6. **Touch edge of strip to blood (don’t smear)**
> 7. Hold until meter beeps / starts countdown
> 
> ---
> 
> ## If it still throws Error 3
> - Try a **different strip vial**
> - Check expiration date
> - Inspect strip port (dust/debris)
> - Ensure meter isn’t cold/hot (room temp ideal)
> 
> ---
> 
> ## When to suspect device issue
> Only after:
> - Multiple strips fail  
> - Different strip batch fails  
> - Proper technique confirmed  
> 
> Then it could be:
> - Strip port damage  
> - Internal sensor fault  
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Reality check (important)
> This error is **almost always user input + strip issue**, not the meter. Don’t waste time assuming the device is broken until you eliminate technique and strip variables.
