### Assistant
> ## Deinterlace (simple, no fluff)
> 
> **Deinterlace = a fix for old-style video.**
> 
> Back in the day (TV/broadcast), video was recorded in **interlaced format**:
> - Each frame is split into **two half-frames (fields)**
> - One shows odd lines, one shows even lines
> - When combined → can cause **horizontal “lines” or jitter** on motion
> 
> ---
> 
> ## What deinterlacing does
> 
> It:
> - Combines those half-frames into a full frame
> - Removes:
>   - Flickering
>   - “Combing” lines on moving objects
> 
> ---
> 
> ## For YOU (important part)
> 
> 👉 Your recordings (phone, dashcam, modern camera) are almost certainly **progressive**, NOT interlaced.
> 
> So:
> 
> ### Set this:
> - **Deinterlace: OFF / Disabled**
> 
> ---
> 
> ## When you would turn it ON
> 
> Only if you see:
> - Horizontal line artifacts on motion
> - Old TV footage
> - DVDs / broadcast recordings
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Bottom line
> 
> - Modern video → **leave it OFF**
> - Old/TV footage → turn it ON
> 
> ---
> 
> If you ever see weird horizontal “scan lines” in motion, that’s when you flip it. Otherwise, ignore it completely.
