> So now the surplus issue has moved from **“argue the amount”** to **“make them deposit the money and get the Clerk to release it.”**
> 
> ## What Now
> 
> ### 1. Check whether FCFCU has deposited the $7,521.37
> 
> The order does **not** directly say “pay Cody.” It says Plaintiff is to submit the money **to the Clerk**. That means the next bottleneck is whether FCFCU actually deposits it.
> 
> Call the Marion County Clerk and ask:
> 
> > “In case 49D03-2501-MF-002559, has Financial Center First Credit Union deposited the $7,521.37 surplus ordered by the Court?”
> 
> Also ask:
> 
> > “What does the Clerk need from me to receive disbursement once the funds are deposited?”
> 
> Get names, dates, and exact answers.
> 
> ### 2. Your filed motion may still matter
> 
> Even though the Court signed Plaintiff’s proposed order, your motion asks for the next missing piece: **immediate disbursement to you.** Keep it on the record. Do not withdraw it.
> 
> Your motion creates a clean record saying:
> 
> - you consented only to speed up disbursement;
> - you are the mortgage debtor/former owner;
> - no other party appears to have claimed the surplus;
> - you reserve all other rights and claims. fileciteturn0file1
> 
> That is useful.
> 
> ### 3. If no money is deposited quickly, file a short compliance notice
> 
> Do **not** file a giant angry motion. File a clean, boring, annoying one. Something like:
> 
> **Defendant’s Notice Regarding Compliance With Order on Motion for Acceptance of Overbid Calculation and Request for Status of Deposit**
> 
> Purpose: tell the Court the order requires Plaintiff to deposit $7,521.37, ask whether the deposit has been made, and ask the Court to direct prompt compliance/disbursement.
> 
> ### 4. If the money is deposited but not released, file a motion to disburse
> 
> Title:
> 
> **Defendant’s Motion for Disbursement of Surplus Funds Deposited With Clerk**
> 
> Keep it narrow. No ADA, no writ, no damages, no fraud, no whole case history. This is a money-release motion only.
> 
> ### 5. Separate claims stay separate
> 
> Do **not** mix property loss, ADA issues, bad service, FCRA, credit reporting, or damages into the surplus lane right now. That muddies the cleanest cash lane.
> 
> Current lane:
> 
> > **Get the $7,521.37 deposited and released.**
> 
> Next lane:
> 
> > **Build the separate demand/regulatory/lawsuit package.**
> 
> ## Immediate Next Move
> 
> Today/tomorrow:
> 
> 1. Check docket for any clerk note or deposit entry.  
> 2. Call Clerk and ask if funds were deposited.  
> 3. If not deposited within a few business days, file a short notice/request for compliance.  
> 4. Once deposited, file or submit whatever the Clerk requires to release the funds to you.
> 
> The order is good news. It locks their number into the record. Now we force the mechanical follow-through.

>

> 
> ## Where to check if they paid
> 
> Check in three places:
> 
> ### 1. Indiana MyCase docket
> 
> Search your case number on **MyCase**. Indiana says MyCase is the public case-search platform for non-confidential case information and many court documents. citeturn729568search1
> 
> Look for docket entries like:
> 
> - “Receipt”
> - “Funds deposited”
> - “Overbid paid”
> - “Clerk receipt”
> - “Payment”
> - “Surplus”
> - “Tendered to Clerk”
> - “Order to disburse”
> - “Check issued”
> 
> But warning: money deposits do **not always show clearly** on the public docket. So do not rely on MyCase alone.
> 
> ### 2. Call Marion County Clerk
> 
> This is the real answer. Call the **Marion County Clerk’s Office** and ask for the civil/finance/bookkeeping side for your case. Marion County’s official site identifies the Clerk’s Office as the county office tied to court case search and court records. citeturn729568search2
> 
> Say:
> 
> > “I’m calling about Marion Superior Court 3, Cause No. 49D03-2501-MF-002559. The Court ordered Financial Center First Credit Union to submit $7,521.37 to the Clerk as surplus/overbid funds. Has that money been received by the Clerk yet?”
> 
> Then ask:
> 
> > “Once received, what exactly do you need from me for disbursement? Do I need a W-9, ID, address confirmation, motion/order, or anything else?”
> 
> Get the person’s name and write down the date/time.
> 
> ### 3. Check your e-filing notifications
> 
> Look for anything from the Clerk or court showing:
> 
> - receipt filed,
> - order processed,
> - notice of deposit,
> - proposed disbursement,
> - rejected/accepted filing,
> - or anything from Plaintiff saying they tendered funds.
> 
> ## Clean next move
> 
> Do this sequence:
> 
> 1. **Call Clerk first.**
> 2. If they say **no deposit yet**, file the revised motion requesting confirmation/compliance/disbursement.
> 3. If they say **yes, funds deposited**, file only a **Motion to Disburse Surplus Funds** and skip the compliance part.
> 4. Email Plaintiff’s counsel after filing, politely asking whether they have submitted the funds to the Clerk.
> 
> Your old motion was good before the order. Now we tighten it because the order changed the posture.
