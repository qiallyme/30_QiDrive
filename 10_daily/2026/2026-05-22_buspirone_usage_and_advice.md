### Assistant
> Yeah — **buspirone is not really a “rescue” anxiety med**, even if the bottle says “3 times daily as needed.”
> 
> It usually works best when taken **consistently**, because it can take **2–4 weeks** to build real anti-anxiety effect. It is **not like hydroxyzine, Xanax, Ativan, etc.** where someone takes it and feels a quick calming hit. citeturn590635search1
> 
> So the practical truth:
> 
> **If she never takes it, it’s basically doing nothing for her baseline anxiety.**  
> Taking one random dose during a panic spiral may not help much and could just make her frustrated because she expects relief.
> 
> For right now, the safest framing is:
> 
> > “Mom, this one isn’t an instant calm-down pill. It only really helps if you take it regularly the way the doctor intended. Since anxiety keeps triggering your breathing, we need to ask the doctor whether this should be scheduled instead of ‘as needed,’ or whether you need a different rescue option.”
> 
> Also watch for side effects if she does take it: **dizziness, nausea, headache, nervousness/lightheadedness** are common enough to matter, especially if she’s already weak or short of breath. citeturn590635search5
> 
> **Do not double up or freestyle it.** Message the hospital/doctor/pharmacist and ask directly:
> 
> > “She has buspirone ordered three times daily as needed, but she rarely takes it. Since it is not fast-acting, should this be scheduled for baseline anxiety, discontinued, or replaced with a true PRN option for acute anxiety/panic that worsens her breathing?”
