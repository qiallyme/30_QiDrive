### Assistant
> No. **Do not write it like “hey judge, I tried to settle, they ignored me, so here’s my next motion, sorry.”**
> 
> That muddies the purpose of the filing and makes you sound reactive.
> 
> ## Better rule
> 
> Your **correction** should do one job only:
> 
> **identify material inaccuracies, omissions, and misleading implications in the March 26 response and correct them with dates, facts, and record-based statements.**
> 
> Keep the settlement point **minimal and procedural**, not emotional and not as evidence of who is right.
> 
> ## Why
> 
> Indiana treats settlement discussions as protected territory. Evidence of compromise offers or negotiations generally is not admissible to prove liability or invalidity of a claim, and Indiana’s ADR framework explicitly ties settlement-negotiation confidentiality to Evidence Rule 408. citeturn298686search2turn298686search0
> 
> So the safer move is:
> 
> - **do not argue:** “they ignored settlement, therefore I win”
> - **do say, if needed:** “despite being afforded an opportunity to resolve the matter without further motion practice, no substantive response was received”
> 
> That frames it as **procedural posture**, not merits proof.
> 
> ## What to say instead
> 
> Use something like this:
> 
> > Defendant submits this correction to address material inaccuracies and omissions in Plaintiff’s March 26, 2026 response.  
> >  
> > To the extent relevant to the present posture, Defendant previously attempted to resolve these issues without further motion practice, but no substantive response was received by the stated deadline. This correction is therefore submitted to ensure the Court is not left with a materially incomplete or misleading record.
> 
> That’s clean.
> 
> ## What not to do
> 
> Do **not** put these in the correction:
> 
> - “sorry Judge”
> - “I tried to settle this once and for all”
> - “they ignored me completely”
> - “here’s my next motion”
> - anything that sounds like a tantrum or threat
> 
> That belongs either:
> - nowhere, if unnecessary, or
> - in a separate executive escalation email, or
> - later in a motion, stated clinically as proof that further informal resolution failed
> 
> ## Structure to use right now
> 
> For the correction, think:
> 
> ### 1. Opening
> “This filing corrects material inaccuracies and omissions in Plaintiff’s March 26 response.”
> 
> ### 2. Numbered corrections
> One point per issue:
> - ownership/public-record timeline
> - misuse of judgment as leverage for retrieval while ignoring service defects
> - security narrative versus actual on-site conduct
> - presence beyond stated time limits
> - anything else that is objectively supportable
> 
> ### 3. Very short posture sentence
> “One effort to resolve this without more motion practice was made; no substantive response was received.”
> 
> ### 4. Close
> “Defendant requests that the Court consider the corrected factual posture in evaluating any further proceedings.”
> 
> ## Bottom line
> 
> **Don’t make the correction a breakup text.**  
> Make it a scalpel.
