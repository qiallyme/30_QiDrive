### Assistant
> Short answer: they *can* talk to your passenger, but they can’t just force it without a legal reason. And your passenger has their own rights separate from you.
> 
> Here’s how it actually breaks down in entity["state","Indiana","U.S. state"]:
> 
> **1. The stop itself**
> If you get pulled over, the officer controls the stop. That includes basic safety stuff—ID, registration, making sure nobody’s a threat.
> 
> **2. Talking to your passenger**
> They’re allowed to *ask* your passenger questions. No problem there.
> 
> But the key difference is:
> - Asking = allowed  
> - Forcing answers = not automatic  
> 
> Your passenger is not required to answer general questions like:
> “Where are you going?”  
> “How do you know the driver?”  
> “What are you doing tonight?”
> 
> They can literally say: “I’d prefer not to answer.”
> 
> **3. ID requirements (important)**
> Indiana is *not* a “stop-and-ID” state for passengers.
> 
> So unless the officer has **reasonable suspicion that the passenger committed a crime**, the passenger:
> - does NOT have to show ID  
> - does NOT have to identify themselves  
> 
> If there *is* reasonable suspicion, that changes things.
> 
> **4. Can police order the passenger out?**
> Yes. Officers can order both driver and passengers out of the vehicle during a traffic stop for safety reasons. That’s been upheld by courts.
> 
> **5. Can they detain the passenger?**
> Only if they have reasonable suspicion or probable cause tied to the passenger—not just because they’re in your car.
> 
> **6. Uber/Lyft context**
> Being a rideshare driver doesn’t give police extra powers.
> 
> But it *does* change how it looks:
> - Random passenger = officer curiosity goes up  
> - Cash ride / off-app = if you say that, it may raise questions (not illegal, just attention-grabbing)
> 
> So it’s not illegal—but it can escalate the conversation.
>

> 
> **Reality check for your situation**
> You’re not wrong—some officers *do* push boundaries and fish for info, especially late at night.
> 
> But legally:
> - You don’t have to answer unrelated questions  
> - Your passenger doesn’t either  
> - You both should stay calm and not escalate
