> - Date
> - Type: Breathing, Pain, Wound, Meds, Appointment, Home Health, Family, Supplies, Money
> - Urgency: Normal, Watch, Urgent
> - Summary
> - Action Needed
> - Status: Open, Waiting, Done
> 
> ### 2. `Action Items`
> This replaces FamilyWall.
> 
> Properties:
> - Task
> - Owner: Cody, Mom, Provider, Home Health, Humana, Pharmacy, Zai
> - Due Date
> - Priority: Now, Today, This Week, Later
> - Status: Not Started, Waiting, Done
> - Related Visit / Note
> 
> ### 3. `Medication Questions`
> This is critical.
> 
> Properties:
> - Medication
> - Question
> - Risk Level: Verify Before Giving, Ask Soon, Info Only
> - Asked To: Pharmacy, PCP, Pulmonary, Pain Mgmt
> - Answer
> - Date Confirmed
> - Status
> 
> First three entries:
> - Prednisone — Which taper is active?
> - Bactrim — Is it safe with sulfa allergy/tachycardia?
> - Norco/Morphine — Can both be used? How far apart?
> 
> ### 4. `Appointments / Calls`
> Properties:
> - Date
> - Provider
> - Purpose
> - Recording / Transcript
> - Summary
> - Decisions
> - Follow-up Tasks
> - Next Appointment
> 
> ### 5. `Provider Contacts`
> Properties:
> - Name
> - Role
> - Organization
> - Phone
> - Fax / Email / Portal
> - Notes

> # Minimum viable workflow
> 
> Every call/visit becomes:
> 
> ## Capture
> Record or use Notion meeting notes.
> 
> ## Summarize
> Use this prompt inside Notion AI:
> 
> “Summarize this care meeting into: what happened, decisions made, medication changes, action items by owner, risks to watch, unresolved questions, and next follow-up.”
> 
> ## Convert
> Turn action items into the `Action Items` database.
> 
> ## Log
> Add a short entry to `Care Log`.
> 
> That’s it. That’s the machine.

> # What not to do

> 
> Supabase is for later when you want:
> - structured app data
> - custom dashboards
> - automations
> - integrations
> - exported care database
