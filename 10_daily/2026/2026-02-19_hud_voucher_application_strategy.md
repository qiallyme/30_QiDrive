### Assistant
> Short answer: **You apply as ONE household. Not two separate applications.**  
> If you try to apply separately you’ll slow things down, create red flags, and possibly mess up the voucher eligibility.
> 
> Now the real strategy.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## First — understand how HUD views this
> Section 8 / Housing Choice Voucher is **household-based**, not individual-based.
> 
> Your mom is the **voucher holder / head of household**.  
> You are a **live-in aide / household member**.
> 
> Those are very different roles in HUD language.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Your role matters a LOT here
> HUD actually has a specific category called **Live-In Aide**.
> 
> A live-in aide is:
> - Someone who lives with a disabled/elderly person
> - Provides care
> - Would not live there otherwise
> - **Is not financially responsible for the household**
> - **Is not counted as income for eligibility**
> 
> That last part is huge.
> 
> If done correctly:
> - Your income usually **does NOT count against her voucher**
> - The household can qualify for a **larger bedroom size**
> - It helps approval odds
> 
> If done incorrectly:
> - Your income can get counted
> - Rent portion increases
> - Approval becomes harder
> 
> So positioning matters.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## The correct application structure
> When applying or updating the voucher:
> 
> Your mom = **Head of Household / Applicant**  
> You = **Request approval as Live-In Aide**
> 
> You do NOT submit a separate voucher application.
> 
> You get added to **her voucher file**.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Why this strategy is the strongest
> Applying separately would imply:
> - Two households
> - Two incomes
> - Possible attempt to get two subsidies for same residence
> 
> Housing authorities hate that. It creates delays and scrutiny.
> 
> Applying as a household with a live-in aide says:
> - Disabled tenant needs caregiver support
> - This is medically necessary housing stability
> 
> That aligns perfectly with HUD rules.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## What you need to do next (practical steps)
> Call or email the housing authority and ask about:
> 
> **“Adding a live-in aide to the voucher household.”**
> 
> They will typically require:
> 
> 1. Doctor letter or verification of disability support need  
>    (simple note saying she benefits from a live-in caregiver)
> 
> 2. Live-in aide request form
> 
> 3. Background check for you
> 
> 4. Statement you are not financially responsible for rent
> 
> This is normal and expected.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Bedroom size strategy (important)
> When a live-in aide is approved:
> 
> You can request a **larger voucher size.**
> 
> Example:
> - Without aide → 1 bedroom voucher  
> - With live-in aide → 2 bedroom voucher
> 
> That dramatically increases housing options.
> 
> Do NOT skip this step.
> 
> Ask specifically:
> **“Will approval of a live-in aide qualify us for a larger bedroom voucher?”**
> 
> ---
> 
> ## The real strategy in plain English
> Don’t try to game the system.  
> **Use the exact lane HUD designed for your situation.**
> 
> Your scenario is textbook:
> - Disabled parent
> - Fixed income
> - Live-in caregiver
> - Shared household
> 
> HUD literally built rules for this.
> 
> ---
