> ## Draft Documentation Note: Financial Harm Caused by Delayed Surplus Funds
> 
> The bank’s handling of the foreclosure surplus funds has caused ongoing and compounding financial harm.
> 
> The property was sold in **August 2025**, and the surplus funds were legally due to me from that sale. Instead of promptly releasing the money, the bank’s delay, obstruction, and failure to properly resolve the surplus forced me to wait until nearly **June 2026** while also requiring me to file unnecessary motions and court documents to recover money that should have already been returned.
> 
> The amount at issue is approximately **$8,000**. This was not speculative money or future income. It was money generated from the sale of my property and legally belonged to me after the foreclosure sale.
> 
> Because those funds were withheld for months, I was deprived of money needed for basic survival and stability, including:
> 
> - purchasing a working phone;
> - maintaining communication access;
> - buying food and basic necessities;
> - paying for transportation and essential needs;
> - stabilizing housing, caregiving, and personal affairs;
> - avoiding additional financial strain caused by lack of access to my own funds.
> 
> The delay did not merely inconvenience me. It created real-world consequences. During this period, I experienced severe stress, food insecurity, and lack of basic resources. These conditions contributed to deterioration in my physical and emotional wellbeing, including episodes of passing out, increased stress-related health concerns, and multiple hospital-related incidents or visits.
> 
> At minimum, the bank’s delay directly worsened my financial instability. The lack of access to funds that were legally mine also indirectly contributed to health and safety concerns because I was unable to meet basic needs that the surplus money would have helped cover.
> 
> The bank should not benefit from delaying payment, forcing unnecessary litigation, and then treating the issue as if the only harm was the unpaid surplus itself. The delay caused additional damages, hardship, and preventable consequences.
> 
> ## Stronger Legal Framing
> 
> The issue is not only that the bank owed me money.
> 
> The issue is that the bank’s conduct deprived me of my own money for approximately **nine to ten months**, forcing me into unnecessary filings, worsening my financial hardship, and contributing to foreseeable harm.
> 
> The bank knew or should have known that withholding surplus foreclosure proceeds from a displaced homeowner could cause serious hardship. These were funds I needed for food, communication, transportation, medical stability, and basic survival. Their delay created consequences beyond the original $8,000.
>

> 
> 2. **Proof of surplus amount**  
>    Court docket, sale distribution, surplus record, bank filings, or clerk records showing approximately $8,000.
>
