## Chat History

### User
> Why the hell is my computer so slow

### Assistant
> Because your computer has decided to emotionally detach from productivity.
> 
> Let’s triage this like adults who don’t have time for nonsense.
> 
> ### First: what kind of slow?
> - Takes forever to boot?
> - Browser lag?
> - Whole system freezing?
> - Just one app (Chrome being dramatic again)?
> 
> If you’re on Windows, here’s the **rapid-fire diagnostic sweep**:
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 1. Task Manager Reality Check  
> Press **Ctrl + Shift + Esc**
> 
> Look at:
> - **CPU**
> - **Memory**
> - **Disk**
> 
> If anything is sitting at 80–100% constantly, that’s your culprit.
> 
> Common offenders:
> - Chrome with 37 tabs you swear you “need”
> - Windows Defender scanning at the worst possible moment
> - OneDrive syncing your entire life
> - Some mystery “Service Host” eating RAM like it pays rent
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 2. Startup Bloat  
> In Task Manager → Startup tab  
> Disable anything that isn’t essential.
> 
> No, you do not need:
> - Spotify launching at boot  
> - Five update managers  
> - That random PDF tool  
> 
> Your machine is not a Times Square billboard.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 3. Storage Check  
> If your SSD is above 85–90% full, performance tanks.
> 
> Open:
> Settings → System → Storage
> 
> If you’re sitting at 20GB free on a 500GB drive, that’s the issue.  
> Computers hate being cramped. They get passive aggressive.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 4. RAM Situation  
> How much RAM do you have?
> 
> - 8GB in 2026 = bare minimum survival mode  
> - 16GB = functional  
> - 32GB = peace  
> 
> If you’re building QiOne, running Supabase, Chrome, and 4 terminals on 8GB… we need to have a serious talk.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 5. Background Updates  
> Windows loves updating at the exact moment you need clarity in life.
> 
> Settings → Windows Update  
> Check if it’s mid-update.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 6. Drive Health  
> If it’s an HDD instead of SSD… that’s it. That’s the reason.  
> Spinning disks belong in museums.
> 
> ---
> 
> ### Now tell me:
> - Windows or Mac?
> - RAM amount?
> - SSD or HDD?
> - Is it slow all the time or just recently?
> 
> We’ll isolate this fast.  
> Because you’ve got too much going on to be bottlenecked by a machine that should be obeying you.
