### Assistant
> You’re thinking about this the right way. Judges hate side-issues that look like rule violations, so getting ahead of it with a simple explanation is smart. The key is **neutral, factual, and short**. Don’t sound defensive and don’t over-explain.
> 
> You basically just want to establish three facts:
> 
> 1. The recording **came from the court’s official record request system**.  
> 2. You **did not record the hearing yourself**.  
> 3. The text format is **your transcription for readability**.
> 
> Here’s a **clean paragraph you can paste into your filing or an affidavit/exhibit introduction**:
> 
> ---
> 
> **Source of Transcript**
> 
> The audio referenced herein was obtained through the court’s official public records request service. The undersigned did not record the hearing independently. The text presented in this exhibit is a transcription prepared by the undersigned from the audio provided through that official records request. The formatting was created solely to present the contents in a clear and readable format for the Court.
> 
> ---
> 
> If you want it **slightly stronger legally**, use this version:
> 
> ---
> 
> **Statement Regarding Source of Hearing Audio**
> 
> The audio of the January 9, 2026 hearing referenced in this filing was obtained through the court’s official public records request service. The undersigned did not record the hearing independently. The transcript included in this exhibit is a transcription prepared by the undersigned from the audio file provided through that official records request. The formatting and organization were created solely to assist the Court in reviewing the content of the hearing.
> 
> ---
> 
> Two tactical notes for you:
> 
> - **Label it as an “Unofficial Transcript.”** Courts care about that wording.  
> - Put something like **“Prepared from official court audio recording obtained via public records request.”** at the top of the exhibit.
> 
> Example header:
> 
> ```
> EXHIBIT B
> Unofficial Transcript
> Prepared from Official Court Audio Recording
> Obtained via Public Records Request
> ```
> 
> That shuts down the “you recorded the hearing” argument before they even try it.
> 
> One more small warning (this matters):  
> When you submit the PDF to e-file, **flatten it and remove comments/annotations**. Indiana e-filing sometimes rejects PDFs or strips metadata weirdly if comments are embedded.
> 
> If you want, I can also show you **a slightly more strategic version that subtly reminds the judge the hearing was officially recorded by the court**, which actually helps your credibility.

### User
> SHOULD I HIGHLIGHT THE IMPORTANT PARTS FOR THIS EXHIBIT

### Assistant
> Short answer: **Yes — but carefully.**
> 
> Judges actually appreciate highlights **if they help them find the key lines fast**, but they **hate documents that look like a neon crime scene**. The trick is controlled highlighting.
> 
> ### Do it like this
> 
> **1. Highlight only the critical sentences**
> Not whole paragraphs. Usually **1–2 lines at a time**.
> 
> Examples of good things to highlight:
> - When the **judge says something important**
> - When **opposing counsel admits something**
> - When something **contradicts the record**
> - Statements about **service, notice, jurisdiction, or surplus funds**
> 
> **2. Use one color**
> Use **light yellow only**.  
> Do **not** use multiple colors unless you're doing a comparison exhibit.
> 
> **3. Add a short note if needed**
> You can add a bracket comment next to the highlight:
> 
> Example:
> 
> > “The Court finds that service was sufficient.”  
> > **[Highlighted – central issue in Motion to Vacate]**
> 
> Keep it minimal.
> 
> ### What NOT to do
> Avoid these common mistakes:
> 
> ❌ Highlighting half the document  
> ❌ Highlighting your own statements instead of the judge's  
> ❌ Using red/green/blue highlights  
> ❌ Adding argumentative comments in the transcript
> 
> ### The clean structure that works best
> 
> At the top of the exhibit:
> 
> ```
> EXHIBIT B
> Unofficial Transcript (Excerpt)
> Prepared from Official Court Audio Recording
> Obtained via Public Records Request
> 
> Note: Limited portions are highlighted for the Court’s convenience.
> ```
> 
> That line **prevents anyone from claiming you altered the transcript**.
> 
> ### Strategic tip (important)
> 
> If the transcript is long, do **not attach the entire thing**.
> 
> Attach:
> 
> **Option A (best)**  
> Relevant pages only with highlights.
> 
> **Option B**  
> Full transcript but **highlight only the key sections**.
> 
> Judges prefer **shorter exhibits**.
> 
> ### One more thing that will make this look professional
> 
> Add **timestamps** if possible:
> 
> Example:
> 
> ```
> [00:14:22] Judge: The Court finds that service was sufficient.
> ```
> 
> That makes it **much harder for opposing counsel to misrepresent context**.
