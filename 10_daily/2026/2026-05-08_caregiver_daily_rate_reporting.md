> Cody, you’re not crazy. They moved you into what sounds like **Structured Family Caregiving / daily stipend / per-diem caregiver pay**, but they failed to give you the operating rules. That is sloppy on their part.
> 
> Here’s the clean version.
> 
> ## What this probably is
> 
> It sounds like they are no longer treating you like an hourly attendant-care worker. They’re treating you like the **primary live-in caregiver** paid a **daily rate** for days your mom is home and services are delivered.
> 
> Indiana FSSA describes Structured Family Caregiving as a **daily rate/per diem** paid when services are delivered that day under an approved service plan. citeturn396341view0
> 
> So no, you may not be expected to clock exact hours like “8:03 AM to 4:17 PM.” But that does **not** mean “no documentation.” It means your documentation should prove:
> 
> 1. Mom was home that day.  
> 2. You were the primary caregiver.  
> 3. Care was actually provided.  
> 4. Any issues, changes, refusals, symptoms, or incidents were noted.  
> 5. If you were away/sick/out of town, coverage was handled properly.
> 
> ## The uncomfortable truth
> 
> Until they give you the exact process, you need to **protect yourself like this could be audited later**.
> 
> Do **not** rely on “they know I’m here.” That’s how people get jammed up later when payroll, Medicaid, agency QA, or a case manager asks, “Where is the daily documentation?”
> 
> Start keeping your own daily log **immediately**, even if nobody asked for it yet.
> 
> ## What to report daily
> 
> Use this simple daily note. This is good enough to cover your ass without turning your life into a damn dissertation.
> 
> **Daily Caregiver Log**
> 
> **Date:**  
> **Client:** Mom’s name  
> **Caregiver:** Cody  
> **Client location:** Home / Hospital / Rehab / Other  
> **Care provided today:**  
> - Personal care: bathing, toileting, dressing, hygiene, transfers, mobility  
> - Meals/fluids: breakfast/lunch/dinner, appetite, hydration  
> - Household support: linens, laundry, dishes, trash, supplies, room safety  
> - Monitoring: breathing, dizziness, nausea, pain, swelling, confusion, falls risk  
> - Appointments/visits: nurse, PT, doctor, calls, pharmacy  
> - Supplies/medication coordination: refills needed, meds on hold, questions for nurse/PCP  
> 
> **Condition today:**  
> Stable / improved / worse / needs follow-up
> 
> **Concerns / changes:**  
> Write anything unusual.
> 
> **Caregiver availability:**  
> Present in home / stepped out briefly / backup caregiver covered / respite used
> 
> **Follow-up needed:**

>

> ## Important nuance: medication
> 
> For your **care continuity notes**, yes, track meds, meds on hold, missed doses, side effects, and questions.
> 
> But for **billing documentation**, be careful. Indiana’s SFC FAQ says medication administration by an immediate family member may be allowed as a normal family role, but it says medication administration is **not itself a Structured Family Caregiving service** and should not be included as SFC service documentation. citeturn396341view0
> 
> Translation:  
> You can write, “Medication question: morning meds caused dizziness/nausea; nurse/PCP follow-up needed.”  
> But don’t frame the whole daily paid service as “I administered meds” unless the agency specifically tells you how they want that documented.
> 
> ## What happens if you’re sick, leave town, or miss a day?
> 
> This is the part they absolutely need to answer in writing.
> 
> Indiana’s SFC FAQ says unskilled respite is included in SFC, up to **15 days per year**, and the provider agency must coordinate/obtain that respite care. citeturn396341view0
> 
> So your questions are valid:
> 
> - If you are sick, who covers?
> - If you leave town, do they send respite?
> - Do you lose that day’s pay?
> - Does backup family coverage count?
> - How do they want respite requested?
> - How much notice is required?
> - What if there’s an emergency?
> - Who receives your daily logs?
> - Is there an app, portal, text, email, or paper form?
> - What happens if a daily log is missed?
> 
> They owe you that. Not vibes. Not “we’ll figure it out.” Written procedure.
