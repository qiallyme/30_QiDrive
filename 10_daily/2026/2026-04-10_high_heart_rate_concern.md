### Assistant
> **Quick Triage**  
> A heart rate of **137 bpm** is **high** for most situations at rest. Whether it’s concerning depends on what you’re doing and how you feel.
> 
> ---
> 
> **Do This Now**
> - Sit upright, relax shoulders, slow your breathing (in through nose, out through pursed lips)
> - Check: are you **at rest or just finished activity/nebulizer?**
> - Recheck pulse after **5–10 minutes of rest**
> - If you use a pulse ox: check oxygen level too
> - Avoid another bronchodilator dose *right now* unless clearly needed
> 
> ---
> 
> **Common COPD-related causes**
> - **Recent nebulizer (albuterol/ipratropium)** → can raise HR
> - **Shortness of breath/anxiety**
> - **Low oxygen**
> - Infection or dehydration
> 
> ---
> 
> **Med Insight**
> - **Albuterol (SABA)**: often causes **fast heart rate, jitteriness**
> - **Trelegy (LABA component)**: mild HR increase possible, but less sudden than neb treatments  
> - Using both close together can **stack effects**
> 
> ---
> 
> **If Not Improving (after ~10–15 min rest)**
> - Try controlled breathing again
> - Check for symptoms:
>   - chest discomfort
>   - dizziness
>   - worsening breathlessness
> 
> ---
> 
> **When to Escalate**
> - Go urgent/ER **now** if:
>   - HR stays **>130 at rest**
>   - chest pain, faintness, or confusion
>   - oxygen is low (if you monitor) or breathing is worsening
> 
> ---
> 
> **Quick question (helps guide this):**
> 1. Were you resting, or did this happen after activity or a breathing treatment?  
> 2. Any symptoms (chest pain, lightheaded, worse shortness of breath)?
