> That burning spot near the anus matters because diaper rash is possible, but a pressure sore, yeast rash, hemorrhoid, skin tear, or infection can start the same way. Treat it like skin-protection plus “watch closely,” not just slap cream and forget it.

### Assistant
> Treat it **now as irritation + pressure risk**.
> 
> ## Immediate safety check
> Look at the spot if you can.
> 
> **Call nurse/doctor/home health today** if you see:
> - Open sore, blister, bleeding, raw skin
> - Purple/dark spot, hard lump, swelling, warmth
> - Drainage, bad smell, pus
> - Fever, new confusion, worsening pain
> - Pain that is deep/sharp or not matching the rash
> - It does not improve in **24–48 hours** after pressure relief and barrier care
> 
> Mayo says warning signs of a bedsore need pressure relief, and if it does not improve in 24–48 hours, contact a healthcare professional; infection signs like fever, drainage, odor, warmth, or swelling need medical care right away. citeturn712150search2
> 
> ## Do this next
> 1. **Clean gently**
>    - Use warm water or gentle wipes.
>    - Avoid alcohol pads on that rash area. Too harsh. Burns like hell and can worsen irritation.
> 
> 2. **Dry completely**
>    - Pat dry, do not rub.
>    - Let it air out a few minutes if possible.
> 
> 3. **Apply barrier cream thick**
>    - Use **zinc oxide diaper rash cream**, **petroleum jelly/Vaseline**, **Aquaphor**, or a moisture barrier cream.
>    - Think “frosting layer,” not lotion.
>    - MedlinePlus says zinc oxide, lanolin, or petrolatum can form a protective barrier for incontinence-exposed skin. citeturn712150search6
> 
> 4. **Change brief/pad more often**
>    - Wetness and stool sitting on skin is the enemy.
>    - MedlinePlus says remove soaked pads/clothing right away, clean and dry the skin, and consider a barrier cream. citeturn712150search7
> 
> 5. **Get pressure off the left cheek**
>    - Have her shift to the other side or slightly reclined/offloaded.
>    - Pillow under one hip/side, not directly under the sore spot.
>    - Check that the brief is not bunched up or rubbing.
> 
> ## What not to do
> - No alcohol pads on the irritated skin.
> - No peroxide.
> - No powder/cornstarch near breathing issues. Inhalation risk, and it can cake up.
> - Do not massage a sore/purple area. If it is pressure-related, rubbing can make tissue damage worse.
> 
> ## Watch for yeast
> If it is **bright red, shiny, moist, spreading, in folds, or has little red “satellite” spots**, it may be yeast. Barrier cream helps protect, but yeast may need an antifungal like clotrimazole/miconazole — verify with pharmacist/doctor because of her complexity.
> 
> ## Quick script for her
> “Your skin is irritated right there, and I’m not arguing with you. I’m protecting it before it turns into a sore. We’re going to clean, dry, put the barrier cream on, and get pressure off that side.”
> 
> ## Care log entry
> **Skin concern:** Burning/pain left buttock/cheek, left of anus. Suspected diaper rash vs pressure irritation.  
> **Action:** Cleaned gently, dried, barrier cream applied, pressure offloaded, brief checked/changed.  
> **Watch:** redness, open skin, swelling, warmth, drainage, odor, fever, worsening pain.  
> **Escalate:** call nurse/home health/doctor if not improved in 24–48 hrs or sooner if infection/open sore signs.
