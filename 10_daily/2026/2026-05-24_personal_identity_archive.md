> # Access Layer Model
> 
> This is the important part. The folders need **privacy levels**, not just topics.
> 
> Use this access ladder:
> 
> | Level | Name | Meaning | Example |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | `L0` | Public | Safe for public/professional use | LinkedIn bio, resume summary |
> | `L1` | Trusted Context | Safe for close collaborators or AI assistants | communication style, work preferences |
> | `L2` | Personal | Private but shareable with chosen people | life history, patterns, ADHD notes |
> | `L3` | Clinical/Sensitive | Provider-level or legal/clinical context | symptoms, trauma summaries, panic notes |
> | `L4` | Locked | Eyes only unless explicitly released | raw journal, unprocessed trauma, private legal/medical |
> | `L5` | Do Not Ingest | Not for AI/search/indexing unless manually authorized | raw private notes, extreme vulnerability |
> 
> That gives you gates without making the system confusing.
