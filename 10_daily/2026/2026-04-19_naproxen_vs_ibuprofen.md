> **Naproxen** is a stronger over-the-counter (and sometimes prescription) pain reliever in the same class as ibuprofen. It’s an NSAID (non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug), meaning it reduces **inflammation**, not just pain.
>

> 
> ## What it actually does
> - Blocks prostaglandins → lowers **inflammation + pain + swelling**
> - Works better than entity["medication","Acetaminophen","pain reliever"] (Tylenol) for inflammatory pain
> - Lasts longer than entity["medication","Ibuprofen","NSAID"]
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Common version you’ll see

> - Brand name: entity["medication","Aleve","naproxen brand"]
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Why it might help when the others didn’t
> If her pain is coming from:
> - joint irritation  
> - nerve compression from positioning  
> - muscle strain from bad alignment  
> 
> → naproxen is usually **more effective** because it hits inflammation harder and stays active longer (8–12 hours vs ~4–6 for ibuprofen).
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Dosing (keep this tight)
> - Typical OTC: **220 mg**
> - Start: **220–440 mg**, then 220 mg every 8–12 hrs
> - Max OTC daily: **660 mg**
