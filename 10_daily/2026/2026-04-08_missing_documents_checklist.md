> - 1" margins on all sides
> - Double-spaced body text
> - Single-space only for block quotes / indented quotations
> - Consecutive page numbers throughout the PDF citeturn633989view3turn633989view4turn876609view0turn876609view2
> 
> **Font**
> Marion’s local rule tells you how the document must be prepared, but it does **not** set a specific font family or point size in that rule section. The safest choice is **Times New Roman, 12 pt**. That is boring, readable, and already an accepted Indiana appellate font at 12-point or larger. Do **not** use script type. citeturn633989view3turn557719search24
> 
> **Caption / heading**
> Your pleading should include:
> - name of the court
> - division and room number
> - case title
> - case number
> - a title that actually identifies what the filing is. Marion also wants titles to delineate each topic included in the pleading. citeturn633989view3
> 
> **Signature block**
> Use a clean pro se signature block with your name, mailing address, email, phone number, and “Defendant, Pro Se.” Marion’s rule specifically requires that pleadings and motions contain an authorized signature and contact information. citeturn668541search16
> 
> **PDF / e-filing output**
> For the file you upload:
> - submit it as a PDF
> - best practice is to **export/save directly to PDF** from Word, not print-and-scan, because that preserves searchable text
> - if you must scan exhibits, use **300 DPI** and apply **OCR**
> - make sure every page is fully viewable and legible
> - do not upload encrypted or password-protected PDFs citeturn876609view0turn876609view3
> 
> **File size**
