### Assistant
> For **eye + sinus infection**, the antibiotics depend on *where the infection actually is* and whether it is bacterial. Don’t self-pick an oral antibiotic for this, especially with heavy eye pus.
> 
> ## Common antibiotics doctors use
> 
> ### **Bacterial eye infection / pink eye**
> Usually treated with **antibiotic eye drops or ointment**, not oral antibiotics.
> 
> Common options include:
> 
> | Situation | Common antibiotic type |
> |---|---|
> | Basic bacterial conjunctivitis | erythromycin ointment, trimethoprim-polymyxin B drops |
> | Contact lens wearer / higher-risk eye infection | fluoroquinolone drops like ofloxacin, ciprofloxacin, moxifloxacin |
> | Suspected chlamydia/gonorrhea eye infection | **systemic/oral or injected antibiotics**, clinician-managed |
> 
> CDC says bacterial pink eye is usually treated with **topical antibiotic drops or ointment**, and contact lens wearers need topical antibiotics plus prompt eye evaluation because of keratitis risk. citeturn684979search1turn684979search6
> 
> ### **Bacterial sinus infection**
> Common options include:
> 
> | Situation | Common antibiotic |
> |---|---|
> | First-line adult bacterial sinusitis | **amoxicillin-clavulanate** |
> | Penicillin allergy or certain situations | **doxycycline** |
> | More complicated/resistant cases | sometimes levofloxacin or moxifloxacin, but these are not casual meds |
> 
> CDC also says many sinus infections do **not** need antibiotics and often improve on their own. Antibiotics are usually considered when symptoms are severe, last more than 10 days, or worsen after initially improving. citeturn684979search9 Merck lists amoxicillin-clavulanate or doxycycline as common treatments when bacterial sinusitis is suspected. citeturn684979search17
> 
> ## The real answer for *your* situation
> 
> With **worsening thick pus from both eyes**, you need **same-day urgent care or an eye clinic**. That is not “let me guess an antibiotic” territory.
> 
> Go **today** if any of these are present:
> 
> - eye pain
> - blurry vision or vision change
> - light sensitivity
> - swelling around the eyelid/eye socket
> - fever
> - severe headache
> - contact lens use
> - one eye looking worse than the other
> - trouble moving the eye
> 
> Do **not** use leftover antibiotics, steroid eye drops, or someone else’s drops. Don’t wear contacts. Wash hands, use warm clean compresses, and don’t share towels/pillowcases.
> 
> Plainly: **oral doxy/amoxicillin might help a sinus infection, but they may not properly treat an eye infection.** The eye likely needs the right **eye antibiotic**, and if it’s severe or related to sinus/orbital infection, that needs medical evaluation fast.
