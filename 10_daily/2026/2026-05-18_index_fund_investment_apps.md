> The strategic pattern should be:

> # QiServer Backup Doctrine
> 
> **Every app with its own database needs two backup layers:**
> 
> | Layer | Purpose | Format |
> |---|---|---|
> | **Raw restore backup** | Rebuild the app exactly after failure | SQL dump + app uploads/config |
> | **Portable data export** | Move/report/analyze data elsewhere | CSV / JSON / Parquet |
> 
> The uncomfortable truth: **CSV exports alone are not enough**. CSV is good for reporting and migration, but not always enough to fully restore an app. You need both.
> 
> ## Don’t use plain FTP
> 
> FTP is crusty and insecure. Use one of these instead:
> 
> | Option | Use |
> |---|---|
> | **SFTP** | Simple file push to another server |
> | **rclone** | Push backups to Drive, S3, Backblaze, Supabase Storage, etc. |
> | **restic** | Encrypted deduplicated backups |
> | **pg_dump / mysqldump** | Database-native backup |
> | **Supabase Storage** | Store backup files |
> | **Supabase Postgres import** | Only for structured mirror tables later |
> 
> ## Best future design
> 
> For each app stack under:
> 
> `/srv/qios/stacks/{app_name}`
> 
> eventually add:
> 
> | File | Purpose |
> |---|---|
> | `backup-{app}.sh` | Dumps database + uploads |
> | `export-{app}.sh` | Creates CSV/JSON exports |
> | `restore-{app}.sh` | Restores from backup |
> | `backup.env` | Destination config, not committed |
> | `backups/` | Local timestamped backups |
> | `README.md` | Restore instructions |
> 
> ## For your apps
> 
> | App | Backup Type |
> |---|---|
> | Firefly III | MariaDB/Postgres dump + uploads |
> | Paperless-ngx | Postgres dump + media + export folder |
> | NocoDB | Postgres dump + attachments |

> | Wiki.js | Postgres dump + uploads/assets |
> | SolidTime | Postgres dump |
> | Qdrant | snapshot/export volume |
> | Neo4j | dump/database backup |
> | Uptime Kuma | sqlite/db volume backup |
> | Portainer | data volume backup |
> 
> ## Supabase as backup
> 
> Use Supabase in two different ways:
> 
> ### 1. Supabase Storage = backup vault  
> Good for:
> 
> - `.sql.gz`
> - `.tar.gz`
> - CSV exports
> - app config snapshots
> - Paperless exports
> 
> ### 2. Supabase Postgres = reporting mirror  
> Good for:
> 
> - unified transaction tables
> - financial dashboards
> - app inventory
> - metadata registry
> - backup index table
> 
> Do **not** try to dump every app’s raw schema into Supabase as-is. That becomes swamp behavior. Use Supabase for **portable normalized exports**, not app resurrection.
> 
> ## Better naming convention
> 
> Use something like:
> 
> `qiserver_{app}_{backup_type}_{YYYYMMDD_HHMMSS}.{ext}`
> 
> Examples:
>

> - `qiserver_firefly_exports_20260518_114500.tar.gz`
> - `qiserver_paperless_media_20260518_114500.tar.gz`
> - `qiserver_n8n_db_20260518_114500.sql.gz`

> ```

> ## Safer option: put Firefly on a path
> 
> If your current Tailscale URL already serves the QiServer UI, use paths instead:
> 
> ```bash
> sudo tailscale serve --https=443 --set-path=/firefly http://127.0.0.1:8087
> sudo tailscale serve --https=443 --set-path=/firefly-importer http://127.0.0.1:8088
> ```
> 
> Then access:
> 
> ```text
> https://qiserver-1.cerberus-sirius.ts.net/firefly
> https://qiserver-1.cerberus-sirius.ts.net/firefly-importer
> ```
> 
> Tailscale’s current CLI supports serving HTTPS or HTTP with `--https=<port>` / `--http=<port>`, and it can reverse-proxy to localhost services. citeturn317605search1

> ## But here’s the catch
> 
> Some apps do not love being served under a subpath like `/firefly`. Firefly may behave better at a root domain than a path.
> 
> So the clean order is:
> 
> 1. Check what Serve is doing now.
> 2. If nothing important is on `443`, serve Firefly at root.
> 3. If your homepage is already on root, either use the path version or later create a cleaner reverse proxy setup.
