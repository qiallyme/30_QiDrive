# Sleep vs Checking Vitals

## Chat History

### User
> Ok so my mom fell asleep it’s not quit time for her pills and she is breathing and was doing really well earlier so I have no reason to worry but I don’t want to disturb her by checking her vitals right now. Should I wait until time for meds and give her dinner then or wake her up now?

### Assistant
> Let her sleep **unless something looks wrong**.
> 
> Right now: **breathing + doing well earlier + not med time yet = do not disturb.** Sleep is medicine too, and waking her early just to “check because anxiety” can create more chaos.
> 
> ## Do this next
> 
> 1. **Visually check from a distance**
>    - Breathing looks regular?
>    - Color looks normal?
>    - Not gasping, choking, gray/blue, sweating heavily, or unusually hard to wake?
>    - Oxygen tubing/equipment in place if she uses it?
> 
> 2. **Wait until pill time**
>    - Then gently wake her.
>    - Offer dinner or a small snack first, especially if any meds should not be taken on an empty stomach.
>    - Check vitals then if she’s awake enough.
> 
> 3. **Do not give pills while she is too sleepy**
>    - If she is groggy, confused, nodding off, or not swallowing safely: pause.
>    - Document it and verify with nurse/pharmacy/doctor if meds are time-sensitive.
> 
> ## Wake her now only if you see red flags
> 
> Wake/check/escalate if there is:
> - Labored breathing, wheezing worse than usual, gasping
> - Blue/gray lips or face
> - Oxygen very low if you happen to see monitor reading
> - New severe confusion or can’t wake normally
> - Chest pain
> - Fall/head injury concern
> - Vomiting/choking
> - Extreme unusual sleepiness after pain meds/sedating meds
> 
> If any of those are present, **don’t debate it — check her and call nurse line/911 depending on severity.**
> 
> ## Care log entry
> 
> **Evening rest check:** Mom fell asleep before scheduled pills/dinner. Breathing observed, no immediate distress noted. She had been doing well earlier. Plan: allow sleep until scheduled medication time, then wake gently, offer dinner/snack, check vitals if awake/appropriate, and give meds only if alert enough to swallow safely.
