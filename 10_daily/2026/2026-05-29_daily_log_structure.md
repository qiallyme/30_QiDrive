### User
> We've got to put together a daily log.

### Assistant
> Good—this is the kind of thing that pays dividends fast if we structure it right.
> 
> Before I build it, I need to pin the intent so we don’t create something you’ll abandon in three days:
> 
> What’s the primary job of this log?
> 
> Are we optimizing for:
> – operational tracking (what got done, blockers, outputs)  
> – personal performance (focus, energy, ADHD patterns)  
> – strategic clarity (decisions, insights, leverage moves)  
> – accountability (proof of progress / reporting)  
> – or all of the above, but weighted?
> 
> Also, how are you planning to use it?
> Quick brain dump? Structured system? Something you’ll review weekly?
> 
> ---
> 
> In the meantime, here’s a **tight, no-bloat baseline** that works for high-output operators like you:
> 
> **Daily Log v1 (lean + useful)**
> 
> **1. Reality Check (2 min)**
> - Top 1–3 priorities for today  
> - What actually matters (not busywork)
> 
> **2. Execution**
> - What I completed (real outputs only)  
> - What moved the needle  
> 
> **3. Friction / Blocks**
> - Where I got stuck  
> - Why (distraction, unclear spec, external dependency, etc.)
> 
> **4. Signal**
> - Insights, patterns, or realizations  
> - Anything that changes how you operate
> 
> **5. Energy + Focus (quick tags)**
> - Focus: High / Medium / Low  
> - Energy: High / Medium / Low  
> - Notes if something impacted it (sleep, stress, etc.)
> 
> **6. Loose Ends**
> - Open loops that need closure  
> - Things to carry into tomorrow
> 
> **7. Tomorrow Setup (1–2 min)**
> - First action to start the day  
> - Anything that removes friction in advance
> 
> ---
> 
> If you want to level this up into a system (which I recommend), we can evolve it into:
> 
> - a **Notion / Obsidian template**
> - auto-linked to projects
> - with weekly review rollups
> - and pattern tracking (your ADHD signals over time)
> 
> But first—tell me:
> Do you want this **minimal and fast**, or **systemized and scalable** from day one?
