### User
> What do I tell my mom she says she is scared but she’s at the hospital how can I get her to calm down without you know it not working

### Assistant
> Tell her **less**, not more. When she’s scared, logic usually bounces off. Use **short, grounding, control-preserving phrases**.
> 
> Say this:
> 
> :::writing{variant="chat_message" id="48291"}
> Mom, you are safe right now. You are already at the hospital, which means help is right there.
> 
> You do not have to figure everything out this second. Your only job right now is to breathe slow, let them check you, and tell the nurse if something feels worse.
> 
> You’re in control. I’m not leaving you to handle this alone. Let’s just do the safest next step first.
> :::
> 
> Then guide her body, not her brain:
> 
> “Put one hand on your belly.”
> “Drop your shoulders.”
> “Breathe in through your nose for 2.”
> “Blow out slow like cooling soup.”
> “Do that with me three times.”
> 
> If she keeps spiraling, say:
> 
> :::writing{variant="chat_message" id="73924"}
> I hear you. I’m not arguing with the fear. But we are not feeding it right now. You are in the safest place you can be. Tell the nurse: “I feel scared and I need help calming my breathing.”
> :::
> 
> And Cody, key thing: **do not over-explain**. Her scared brain will turn details into more things to panic about. Repeat the same anchor like a broken record:
> 
> “You’re safe. Help is there. One breath. One step.”
