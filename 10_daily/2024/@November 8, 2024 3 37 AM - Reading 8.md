### 4. Interpretation

**Here’s an interpretation for each additional card you drew, with respect to the clarity and guidance questions asked:**

1.    **Universe (Ten of Wands) – Clarified by Death Reversed**

**With the Ten of Wands indicating a heavy burden, the appearance of Death reversed as clarification suggests that part of the challenge here is an inability to let go of something—perhaps a habit, a past experience, or an old way of thinking—that’s weighing your friend down. This card in reverse often points to resistance to transformation or fear of change. The universe may be nudging them to release what no longer serves them, even if it’s uncomfortable, in order to make space for a lighter, freer path. It’s an encouragement to recognize and confront any fear or resistance to change so that they can let go of unnecessary burdens.**

2.    **Soul (Three of Wands Reversed) – Clarified by Queen of Wands**

**The Queen of Wands here provides a warm, encouraging energy to the soul’s journey. While the Three of Wands reversed can indicate frustration or uncertainty about moving forward, the Queen of Wands brings a message of confidence, self-assuredness, and bold action. This suggests that your friend’s soul craves a sense of purpose and the courage to act on their desires. The Queen’s energy encourages them to tap into their inner confidence, creativity, and passion to overcome self-doubt. It’s a reminder to trust in their own charisma and vision, embodying the vibrant and magnetic qualities of the Queen of Wands to push past any blocks.**

3.    **Moon (Strength) – Clarified by Ace of Cups Reversed**

**The Ace of Cups reversed, in the context of Strength, indicates that emotional healing and self-compassion are needed. This card often signifies repressed emotions or a blocked flow of self-love and acceptance. In the Strength position, it suggests that to truly face and overcome anxieties, your friend may need to turn inward and address any emotional wounds or unresolved feelings. The reversed Ace of Cups encourages them to allow themselves to feel and process these emotions, letting go of anything that might be holding them back from experiencing love, both for themselves and others. It’s a call to nurture their own emotional well-being and open up to the healing that comes from releasing pent-up feelings.**

4.    **Earth (Page of Wands Reversed) – Clarified by Four of Wands**

**The Four of Wands is a card of celebration, stability, and community, and it provides a positive counterbalance to the uncertainty of the Page of Wands reversed. In the earthly, practical realm, this suggests that grounding themselves in supportive environments or leaning on close relationships can help bring stability. The Four of Wands encourages finding a secure foundation—whether in family, friends, or a stable project—that can provide the encouragement and resources needed to act on their ideas. It may also signify that once your friend feels rooted and supported, they’ll have the clarity and confidence to move forward on the practical aspects of their goals.**

5.    **Sun (The Sun) – Clarified by Six of Wands**

**The Six of Wands, a card of victory, public recognition, and self-confidence, is a wonderful guide for reaching the positive energy of the Sun. This card suggests that your friend’s journey to happiness and fulfillment can be achieved through self-belief and taking pride in their accomplishments. The Six of Wands encourages celebrating wins, both big and small, and allowing themselves to feel deserving of recognition and success. In essence, it’s saying that embracing their strengths, being visible in their efforts, and cultivating confidence in their own abilities will guide them to that place of joy and clarity embodied by the Sun.**

**Summary Interpretation**

**This spread of clarifying cards suggests a transformative journey where your friend is encouraged to let go of old burdens (Death Reversed), embrace their inner confidence (Queen of Wands), confront emotional blockages with compassion (Ace of Cups Reversed), find stability in supportive relationships or communities (Four of Wands), and celebrate their victories along the way (Six of Wands). The path to reaching the Sun appears to involve both inner and outer work—releasing past burdens, nurturing their sense of self, facing hidden emotions, and leaning on a supportive foundation. By embracing these aspects, they can ultimately achieve the clarity, joy, and success that the Sun promises.**
