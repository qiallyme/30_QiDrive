Bench warrants are typically issued for individuals who have violated the terms of their release, failed to pay fines or child support, or disregarded a court summons. If you have a bench warrant against you, it's important to consult with an attorney to understand your legal rights and options.

### Assistant
> Certainly! Here are some additional details about bench warrants:
> 
> 1. Issuance: Bench warrants are issued by a judge from the bench, hence the name. They are typically issued when someone fails to comply with a court order or fails to appear in court as required.
> 
> 2. Purpose: The purpose of a bench warrant is to compel the person's appearance in court. It authorizes law enforcement to arrest the individual and bring them before the judge.
> 
> 3. Types of Violations: Bench warrants can be issued for various reasons, including failure to pay fines or child support, non-compliance with probation conditions, failure to appear for a scheduled court hearing, or failure to respond to a subpoena.
> 
> 4. Arrest and Detention: Once a bench warrant is issued, law enforcement officers have the authority to arrest the person named in the warrant. The individual can be detained and held in custody until they are brought before the court.
> 
> 5. Recalling a Bench Warrant: It is possible to have a bench warrant recalled or lifted. This typically requires taking the necessary legal steps, such as hiring an attorney to file a motion with the court. However, the specific process may vary depending on the jurisdiction and the circumstances of the case.
> 
> It's important to note that laws regarding bench warrants can vary between jurisdictions, so it's advisable to consult with a legal professional for personalized advice if you are facing or concerned about a bench warrant.
