> ## Cancellation SOP for Bookkeeping Clients 
> 
> ### Steps to Take: 
> 
> 1. **Initial Contact**: Once a client has indicated they wish to cancel their services, reach out to them immediately (within 24 hours if possible). 
> 
> 2. **Set Up a Meeting**: Schedule a meeting/call with the client. The goal is to have a conversation about their reasons for cancellation. This should be a listening exercise, not a sales pitch to get them to stay.
> 
> 3. **Prepare for the Meeting**: Review the client's file, understand their history with your company, and their reasons for signing up in the first place. 
> 
> 4. **During the Meeting**: 
>     - Let them express their reasons for cancellation. 
>     - Ask open-ended questions to understand their concerns better.
>     - Try to address any fixable issues or misunderstandings.
>     - If there's no chance of retention, proceed with the cancellation in a respectful and understanding manner.
> 
> 5. **Score Gathering**: 
>     - Ask the client to score their overall experience with your company.
>     - Ask them to score their experience in the last 90 days.
>     - Document these scores for future reference and to help identify potential areas for improvement.
> 
> 6. **Finalizing Cancellation**: 
>     - Have them sign the cancellation agreement. Make sure they understand the clauses that survive a cancellation.
>     - Process their cancellation in your system.
>     - Ensure all client data is handled as per your company's data retention policy.
> 
> 7. **Post Cancellation**: Send them a farewell email thanking them for their business, expressing regret over their departure, and letting them know they're welcome back anytime. 
> 
> ### Questions to Ask:
> - What prompted you to consider cancelling our services?
> - Can you share any specific instances or experiences that led to this decision?
> - Did our services meet your expectations?
> - If you could change anything about our services, what would it be?
> - Is there anything we could have done differently to prevent this cancellation?
> - On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate your overall experience with us?
> - On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate your experience with us in the last 90 days?
> 
> ## Cancellation Agreement 
> 
> The cancellation agreement should be created by a legal professional as it has to meet certain legal standards and requirements, depending on the jurisdiction you operate in. However, a general template may look something like this:
> 
> ```Spanish
> Acuerdo de Cancelación
> 
> Por la presente confirmo que deseo cancelar los servicios de bookkeeping proporcionados por Servicios Rice-Velasquez.
> 
> Entiendo y acepto que:
> 
> 1. Todas las obligaciones financieras pendientes hacia Servicios Rice-Velasquez deberán ser saldadas.
> 2. Cualquier cláusula en nuestro acuerdo inicial que se haya especificado para sobrevivir a una cancelación seguirá en efecto. Esto puede incluir, pero no está limitado a, las obligaciones de confidencialidad y las restricciones de no competencia.
> 3. Servicios Rice-Velasquez retendrá mis datos de acuerdo con su política de retención de datos.
> 
> Nombre del Cliente: __________
> 
> Firma del Cliente: __________
> 
> Fecha: __________
> ```
> 
> This should give you a starting point. Always make sure to consult with your legal counsel when creating or revising legal agreements.
