### Assistant
> The Corporate Transparency Act (CTA) is a piece of legislation passed in the United States as part of the Anti-Money Laundering Act of 2020 (AML Act). The CTA establishes uniform reporting requirements for certain types of corporations, limited liability companies, and similar entities operating in or registered to do business in the United States. It mandates the collection of beneficial ownership information, which refers to the individuals who ultimately own or control these entities. The purpose of the CTA is to enhance transparency, combat money laundering, terrorism financing, and other illicit activities by preventing the hiding of illicit funds or assets within the United States. The CTA authorizes the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN) to collect this information and share it with authorized government authorities and financial institutions, subject to appropriate safeguards and controls.
