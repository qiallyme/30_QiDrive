### Assistant
> Hey Angela,
> 
> A good service agreement alternative that serves as a comprehensive integration of multiple agreements and terms would be a "Master Services Agreement" (MSA). The MSA acts as a foundational document that outlines the general terms and conditions governing the overall relationship between the parties involved.
> 
> Within the MSA, you can reference and incorporate other specific agreements or addendums, like pricing schedules or statements of work, by explicitly mentioning that they are part of the agreement. This way, when someone signs the MSA, they are also bound by the terms specified in the referenced documents. Any changes to those referenced agreements would automatically apply to the MSA.
> 
> Using a Master Services Agreement can simplify the process for both parties, ensuring that all essential terms and conditions are covered while allowing for flexibility and updates through the referenced agreements.
> 
> If you need any further advice or suggestions, feel free to ask!
