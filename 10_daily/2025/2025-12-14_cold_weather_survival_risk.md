### Assistant
> You’re not being dramatic. In **3°F** air (and effectively **much colder if you’re exposed to wind/drafts), the *math* is that both **your body** and an **EV battery pack** are on the same side of physics: losing usable energy fast.
> 
> ## 1) What cold does to lithium-ion EV batteries (and why that matters for survival)
> Cold slows battery chemistry and increases internal resistance, so you get:
> - **Less usable range/energy** (often **~20–40% loss** in real-world winter conditions; it can be worse in extreme cold and with heavy cabin heat). citeturn0search3turn0search11  
> - **Higher “overhead” for heat**: cabin heating and battery conditioning can consume a meaningful chunk of your remaining charge, especially when you’re not driving much. citeturn0search11turn0news39  
> 
> Net: if you’re parked using heat as life support, your “time-to-empty” can shrink **a lot** vs what the battery % makes you feel.
> 
> ## 2) How fast hypothermia can set in if the car goes cold
> **Hypothermia = core temp < 95°F** and it’s a medical emergency. citeturn0search9turn0search1  
> The scary part is that hypothermia **impairs thinking and judgment**, so people often don’t realize how bad they’re getting until they can’t respond effectively. citeturn0search1turn0search5  
> 
> ### The honest answer on timing
> There is **no single “X minutes”** number I can responsibly give, because it depends on:
> - clothing/blankets and insulation
> - whether you’re wet/sweaty
> - body size/body fat, exhaustion, calories
> - airflow/drafts (cracked window, leaky seals)
> - whether the cabin starts warm and how fast it equilibrates to outside temp
> 
> But in your scenario—**single digits with severe wind chill, sleeping, limited ability to notice decline, and a dog**—hypothermia can become a **hours-not-days** problem once the cabin loses heat. The danger is not just discomfort; it’s that you can drift from “cold” → “confused” → “can’t act” without a clean wake-up moment. citeturn0search1turn0search9  
> 
> ## 3) What about the dog?
> Dogs also get hypothermia. Smaller, short-haired, wet, or thin dogs lose heat faster. The key practical point: **if you’re cold, your dog is at risk too**, and they may not reliably “alert” you in time.
> 
> ## 4) What you should do tonight (practical, not theory)
> If you are **at any real risk of the car dying while you sleep**, treat that as an emergency planning problem, not a comfort problem.
> 
> **Do this in order:**
> 1. **Do not sleep “hoping the battery holds.”** Make a safer plan *before* you get drowsy.
> 2. **Find a warming option immediately:** hotel lobby, 24-hour place, warming center, hospital ER waiting room if you have no other option. CDC and Ready.gov both emphasize cold can become dangerous quickly and hypothermia is an emergency. citeturn0search1turn0search14  
> 3. In the U.S., **call 2-1-1** for local warming centers/shelters (or if you feel you’re in immediate danger, **911**).
> 4. If you must stay in the car short-term:
>    - **Insulate first, heat second**: layer clothing, wrap in blankets/sleeping bag, cover head/neck.
>    - Avoid sweating (sweat = faster heat loss).
>    - **Use seat heaters over cabin heat** if available (more efficient for warming *you* than warming air).
>    - Set **multiple alarms** (every 20–30 minutes) *and* a “must-check” routine (battery %, cabin temp, your coordination/mental clarity).

> ### The key fact
> A static host like **Cloudflare Pages will NOT automatically list the contents of a folder** unless there’s an app/server generating a directory index. It will only serve files you link to directly.
> 
> So:
> - `.../02_Exposure_and_Harm/` will load **only if** there’s an `index.html` in that folder.
> - Even if there *is* an `index.html`, the browser will **not** magically populate “files in this folder.” HTML can’t list the filesystem on a static host unless you generate that list yourself.
> 
> That’s why you see the folder page but **no .md or .pdf links**.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## What you need: explicit links (2 best options)
> 
> ### Option A (fast + no tooling): “Manual link list” per folder

>   <li><a href="./Cold_Risk_Report.pdf">Cold Risk Report (PDF)</a></li>
