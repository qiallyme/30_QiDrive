### Assistant
> Short answer: **you’re very close, but it is not quite correct yet** for Indiana practice. A few structural and technical fixes are required to avoid giving opposing counsel easy procedural ammo.
> 
> Below is a **direct, no-nonsense breakdown**.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## What You Got Right
> 
> - Proper **caption** (court, cause number, parties) ✔  
> - Clear identification of affiant ✔  
> - Numbered factual paragraphs ✔  
> - Sworn statement language ✔  
> - Purpose tied to service/notice ✔  
> 
> Substantively, this is solid. Procedurally, it needs tightening.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## What Is Incorrect or Risky (and How to Fix It)
> 
> ### 1. **You Cannot Have Two Venue Headers**
> You currently have **two “State / County” sections**:
> - One in the case caption (correct)
> - One under the affidavit title (problematic)
> 
> **Fix:**  
> For Indiana filings, **only the affidavit needs the venue**, and it should match where the affidavit is executed—not the court.
> 
> ✅ Keep **only one**, under the affidavit heading.
> 
> ---
> 
> ### 2. **“ADA / Disability Affidavit” Is Strategically Risky**
> Courts do not reject it—but opposing counsel may argue:
> - Legal conclusions
> - Advocacy rather than facts
> 
> **Safer title (recommended):**
> > **AFFIDAVIT OF [PROVIDER NAME], [CREDENTIALS] REGARDING DISABILITY-RELATED NOTICE LIMITATIONS**
> 
> This signals neutrality and medical facts, not argument.
> 
> ---
> 
> ### 3. **Legal Conclusions Must Be Framed as Medical Opinion**
> Phrases like:
> - “This constitutes excusable neglect”
> - “Necessary accommodation”
> 
> These are **legal determinations**, not medical facts.
> 
> **Fix:**  
> Reframe as **clinical causation**, not legal labeling.
> 
> Example:
> > “From a clinical perspective, these impairments materially interfere with the ability to perceive, prioritize, and act upon irregular or non-redundant forms of notice.”
> 
> Let **you** argue excusable neglect elsewhere.
> 
> ---
> 
> ### 4. **Verification Language Needs Indiana-Correct Form**
> Indiana prefers explicit perjury language under **IC 35-44.1-2-1**.
> 
> Your current verification is close but soft.
> 
> **Use this instead (recommended):**
> > “I affirm under the penalties for perjury under the laws of the State of Indiana that the foregoing representations are true and correct to the best of my knowledge and belief.”
> 
> ---
> 
> ### 5. **You Are Missing the Notary Block**
> Unless this is an **unsworn declaration under IC 34-37-2-1**, you need notarization.
> 
> Right now, it is **neither**.
> 
> You must choose **one**:
> 
> #### Option A — Notarized Affidavit (most courts prefer)
> Add a notary acknowledgment.
> 
> #### Option B — Unsworn Declaration
> Replace “being first duly sworn” language and add statutory declaration wording.
> 
> Given the stakes, **Option A is safer**.
> 
> ---
>
