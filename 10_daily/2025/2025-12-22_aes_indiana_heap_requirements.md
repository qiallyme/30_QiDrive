### Assistant
> Short version first, because this is getting sloppy on AES’s side:
> 
> **AES Indiana does NOT have the legal right to demand screenshots of a HEAP application or personal data.**  
> A **HEAP confirmation number + agency verification** is sufficient under Indiana practice. What they are asking for is unnecessary, unsafe, and arguably improper.
> 
> Now let’s break this down cleanly.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 1. What Indiana HEAP actually requires (and who verifies it)
> 
> In Indiana, HEAP is administered through **local Community Action Agencies (CAA)** under the Indiana Housing & Community Development Authority (IHCDA).
> 
> Key points:
> - The **CAA**, not AES, determines eligibility.
> - Once an application is submitted, the applicant receives a **confirmation number**.
> - The **utility company receives verification electronically or through agency confirmation**, not raw application data.
> 
> AES’s role is **passive**:
> - They apply protections once HEAP status is confirmed.
> - They do **not** audit applications.
> - They do **not** collect HEAP paperwork.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 2. What AES Indiana is legally entitled to ask for
> 
> **They may ask for ONE of the following:**
> - HEAP confirmation number  
> - Name of the administering agency  
> - A verification letter from the agency (if available)
> 
> **They may NOT demand:**
> - Screenshots of the application portal  
> - Copies of Social Security numbers  
> - Household member details  
> - Children’s information  
> - Pregnancy status documentation  
> - Page-by-page application proof
> 
> That would be:
> - Excessive
> - A privacy violation
> - Outside their statutory role
> - Potentially a consumer protection issue
> 
> If they truly needed verification, **they would contact the agency directly**, which they know how to do.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 3. Why their “screenshot” demand is a problem
> 
> There is **no single “screenshot” that proves a HEAP application**.  
> The portal ends with exactly what you described: **a confirmation number**.
> 
> So asking for:
> > “a screenshot of the application”
> 
> …is meaningless unless they are fishing for:
> - SSNs
> - Children’s identities
> - Household composition
> - Immigration-related data (which is especially sensitive here)
> 
> That’s a hard no.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## 4. What this looks like legally (plain truth)
> 
> This behavior falls into:
> - **Improper documentation demand**
> - **Unlawful barrier to utility protections**
> - **Disparate impact** on low-income households with children
> - Potential **IUCC / IURC complaint territory**
> - Possibly **consumer protection** if disconnection pressure continues
> 
> Especially given:
> - Five minor children
> - Pregnancy
> - Active HEAP filing
> - Cold-weather vulnerability
> 
> They are skating on thin ice.
> 
> ---
>

>

>

>
