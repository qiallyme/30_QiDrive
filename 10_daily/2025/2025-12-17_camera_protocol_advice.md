### Assistant
> Short, direct, and practical. Do **not** overthink this.
> 
> ### What to do about the cameras — right now
> 
> **1. Assume every camera is recording.**  
> Doorbell cams, exterior cams, interior cams, motion sensors. Act accordingly.
> 
> **2. You are allowed to be there only for the authorized purpose.**  
> If this is an access window / supervised retrieval:
> - Do **only** what you are permitted to do.
> - No wandering.
> - No commentary.
> - No confrontations.
> 
> **3. Do not touch, cover, unplug, or disable any camera.**  
> That can be framed as:
> - Tampering
> - Trespass escalation
> - Evidence of “bad intent”
> 
> Even something innocent can be spun against you.
> 
> **4. Do not speak to the cameras.**  
> No explanations. No sarcasm. No emotional processing. Silence is safest.
> 
> **5. If you need to communicate, do it out loud and factual.**  
> Example (only if necessary):
> > “I am here solely to retrieve my personal property.”
> 
> That’s it. No extras.
> 
> **6. Assume audio may be recording.**  
> Many doorbells record audio. Act like a court reporter is transcribing.
> 
> **7. Document *yourself*, quietly.**  
> If allowed:
> - Timestamped notes on your phone
> - Photos of *your belongings only*
> - Photos of any damage or missing items  
> Do **not** photograph cameras or security equipment.
> 
> **8. If anyone confronts you:**  
> Say this, verbatim:
> > “I am complying with the authorized access and will continue doing so.”
> 
> Repeat once if needed. Then stop talking.
> 
> ---
> 
> ### Bottom line
> Cameras are **not your enemy** if you behave cleanly.  
> They become **your evidence** if anyone lies later.
> 
> If you want, next we can:
> - Script exactly what to say if stopped  
> - Build a post-visit incident log entry  
> - Decide what *not* to retrieve today strategically
> 
> Tell me which one you need.
