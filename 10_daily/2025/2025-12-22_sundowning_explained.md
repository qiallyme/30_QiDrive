> ## What “sundowning” actually is
> **Sundowning** is a pattern where **confusion, agitation, anxiety, irritability, or cognitive overload intensifies in the late afternoon or evening**. It’s most commonly discussed in dementia contexts, but that framing is incomplete and honestly misleading.
> 
> This is not just a “dementia thing.”
> 
> ## The real mechanism (stripped of fluff)
> At its core, sundowning is a **regulation failure** caused by the convergence of four forces:
> 
> 1. **Circadian rhythm drop**
>    - Cortisol and alertness dip.
>    - Melatonin starts rising.
>    - Executive function weakens.
> 
> 2. **Cognitive fatigue**
>    - The brain’s “buffer” is spent.
>    - Masking, decision-making, emotional regulation cost more than you have left.
> 
> 3. **Sensory + environmental shift**
>    - Lighting changes.
>    - More shadows, more noise, less predictability.
>    - The nervous system reads this as threat, not coziness.
> 
> 4. **Reduced scaffolding**
>    - Fewer people around.
>    - Fewer external cues.
>    - Less structure = more internal chaos.
> 
> When those hit together, the brain **loses top-down control**. Emotions, fears, irritability, looping thoughts, and distortions take the wheel.
> 
> That’s sundowning.
> 
> ## Why it shows up hard in people like you
> Based on everything you’ve shared across time, this tracks:
> 
> - ADHD → executive function depletion by evening  
> - Anxiety / trauma → nervous system already primed  
> - Prolonged stress + displacement → no recovery window  
> - Hyper-responsibility → you burn hot all day  
> - High insight → you *notice* the unraveling in real time (which makes it worse)
> 
> So this isn’t “you losing it.”
> It’s **a nervous system hitting its daily limit**.
> 
> ## What sundowning is NOT
> Let’s kill some bad narratives fast:
> 
> - ❌ Not a character flaw  
> - ❌ Not weakness  
> - ❌ Not “being dramatic”  
> - ❌ Not something you can brute-force with willpower  
> 
> Trying to “push through” sundowning is like revving an engine with no oil.
> 
> ## How it typically presents (checklist)
> If these spike after ~4–7pm, you’re seeing it:
> 
> - Racing or looping thoughts  
> - Emotional amplification (anger, grief, fear)  
> - Catastrophizing  
> - Decision paralysis  
> - Heightened sensitivity to tone, light, sound  
> - Urge to solve *everything right now*  
> - Feeling misunderstood or alone even if you’re not  
> 
> ## The hard truth
> Evening is **not your thinking time**.
> 
> If you keep trying to:
> - plan,
> - argue,
> - file,
> - confront,
> - decide,
> - or emotionally process big things at night,
> 
> you are setting yourself up to spiral.
> 
> That’s not a mindset issue. That’s biology.
> 
> ## The strategic response (this matters)
> Sundowning requires **containment, not insight**.
> 
> **What helps:**
> - Predictable evening routine (boring is good)
> - Lower lighting before sunset
> - Fewer decisions after a set hour
> - Warm food, protein, hydration
> - Body-based regulation (heat, shower, stretching)
> - External reassurance (written notes > thinking)
> - Parking big thoughts for morning review
> 
> **What makes it worse:**
> - Legal strategy
> - Trauma review
> - Family conversations
> - Texting people you’re emotionally activated about
> - “Just one more thing”
> - Doom-scrolling
> - Trying to explain yourself
> 
> ## Reframe this correctly
> Sundowning is your system saying:
> 
> > “I protected you all day. I’m done now.”
> 
> Listening to that signal is not giving up.
> It’s **playing the long game**.
