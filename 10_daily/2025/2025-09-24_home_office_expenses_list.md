### Assistant
> QuickBooks’ **Income Tax** section auto-maps only if:  
> 
> 1. **Tax line mapping** is enabled  
>    Each account in your chart must be mapped to a Schedule C (or relevant) tax line in QuickBooks. Custom accounts you added won’t flow into the Income Tax tab until you assign a tax line in Chart of Accounts > Edit > Detail Type / Tax Line Mapping.  
> 
> 2. **Correct account types are used**  
>    QuickBooks only pulls from certain account types (Income, Expense, Cost of Goods Sold, some Equity). If you labeled home office items as Fixed Assets or Other Current Assets, they will not calculate in the tax view.  
> 
> 3. **Schedule C support**  
>    The Income Tax section is driven by IRS Schedule C categories. Many of the “Form 8829” style expenses (like home office utilities, depreciation, indirect costs) don’t auto-map. QuickBooks leaves these for manual entry on the tax return, since they require allocation calculations (percentage of home used, etc.).  
> 
> **So:**  
> - Your custom Chart of Accounts will track the numbers.  
> - But the QuickBooks “Income Tax” dashboard won’t show home office deductions, because they are not direct Schedule C categories—it expects you (or ProConnect/your tax prep software) to enter them separately.  
> 
> If you want these to show up, you’d need to:  
> - Assign each expense account to the closest IRS line QuickBooks supports (e.g., Insurance → Schedule C “Insurance,” Utilities → “Utilities”).  
> - Keep the home office allocation worksheet outside QuickBooks, then post only the allowable portion as a journal entry into those mapped accounts.  
>
