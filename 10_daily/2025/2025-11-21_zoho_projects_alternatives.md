> can this later be connected to the client portal so what i add for the3m shows up in there kb

### Assistant
> Yes, absolutely. And it’s not even hard, assuming you don’t sabotage yourself with a thousand “nice-to-haves.”
> 
> Think of it like this:
> 
> - **tududi = your internal task engine**
> - **Client Portal = filtered window into that engine**
> - **Client KB = the narrative/plan that tasks attach to**
> 
> ### How the connection should work (clean version)
> 
> 1. **Every task gets a `client_id` and maybe a `kb_node_id`**
>    - `client_id`: Blanca, Luis, ZJK, etc.
>    - `kb_node_id`: optional link to a specific KB page/section (like “DS-260 / Evidence / Translations”).
> 
> 2. **Your Client Portal pulls tasks by `client_id`**
>    - Clients see **only their tasks**, no login to tududi needed.
>    - Read-only by default so clients can’t break your life.
> 
> 3. **The KB shows an embedded “Tasks for this section” view**
>    - You drop a small widget or iframe-like component in the KB page that calls:
>      - `GET /tasks?client_id=blanca&kb_node_id=imm.02`  
>    - Result: that KB page always shows the live checklist under it.
> 
> So when you add tasks internally, they immediately appear:
> - on your board
> - on the client’s portal view
> - under the relevant KB section
> 
> ### The data flow (human words)
> 
> You add task → task saved in tududi DB → client portal reads same DB → KB page renders tasks linked to that page.
> 
> Nothing duplicated. No copy/paste. No “syncing” headaches. One source of truth.
> 
> ### What you’ll need to tweak in tududi
> 
> tududi is simple, so you add two fields:
> 
> - `client_id` (string)
> - `kb_node_id` (string, nullable)
> 
> Then:
> - add a client filter dropdown in your UI
> - expose a tiny API route for client views
> - lock it down with a view token so nobody can guess URLs and snoop
> 
> ### Why this is the right move
> 
> - **You stay in control.**
> - **Clients get visibility without messing with your system.**
> - **Your KB becomes a living deliverable instead of a static doc.**
> - **You can bill off task logs easily later.**
> 
> If you build it this way, you’re basically making Zoho Projects obsolete for your use case, but without the corporate sludge.
