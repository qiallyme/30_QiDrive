> ## 1) Reality check: “my hourly fee” is usually not recoverable in *this* foreclosure case
> In most cases, **a self-represented (pro se) litigant cannot recover “attorney’s fees” for their own time** the way a lawyer would bill. The U.S. Supreme Court has taken that position under common fee-shifting statutes (e.g., §1988). citeturn0search2turn0search14  
> Indiana courts follow the American Rule generally (each side pays their own), unless a statute/contract/bad-faith exception applies. citeturn0search12turn0search8  
> Indiana reporting also reflects that **pro se litigants typically can recover costs/expenses but not “attorney fees”** just because they spent time. citeturn0search9
> 
> **Translation:**  
> - If you’re trying to file something *in the existing foreclosure case* asking for “$X for my hours,” that will likely get resisted hard and may not be awarded.  
> - If you’re building a **damages record** for later (ADA/Title II, civil rights, consumer protection, wrongful eviction/removal, etc.), then documenting your time as **economic loss** is smart.
> 
> So: keep this as a **damages ledger + declaration support**, and use it tactically (settlement leverage + later claims), not as a “please pay my billable rate” request in the foreclosure docket—unless your lawyer later finds a clean statutory hook.
