> ### QuNote is not a list—it’s a lattice.

> 

> ADHD is not a disorder of attention; it’s a difference in dimensionality. Traditional systems force thoughts into timelines, buckets, or hierarchies. QuNote acknowledges that every idea, task, or feeling exists at the intersection of multiple contexts. It reflects how ADHD minds operate: associatively, intuitively, and non-linearly.

> 

> ### QuNote helps users:

> 

> - Capture everything without judgment or order

> - Connect seemingly unrelated thoughts into actionable webs

> - Visualize their inner world as an interconnected system

> - Organize without abandoning their native flow state

> - Transition between structure and spontaneity effortlessly

> 

> > “Every node is part of the whole, and the whole is present in every node.”

> > 

> 

> ### Foundational principles:

> 

> - **Quantum Cognition** — Thoughts are not discrete tasks but potentialities. Until acted upon, they exist in superposition.

> - **Node-Based Structure** — All input (tasks, ideas, events, feelings) are QNodes: contextual, taggable, and relational.

> - **Triadic Entanglement** — Each node is connected by up to three vectors: Entanglement (Why), Orbit (Where), and Entanglement (How). These define a node’s meaning.

> - **Time is Fluid** — Nodes have timestamps, but relationships matter more than chronology.

> - **You Are the Observer** — Observation collapses potential into clarity. Observing a node shapes its trajectory.

> 

> A QNode is the atomic unit of the QuNote system—a snapshot of awareness that lives and evolves through relationships.

> 

> ### Each QNode contains:

> 

> - **Title (What)** — Core identifier

> - **Entanglement Node (Why)** — Single relationship; the parent or trigger of the node (one-to-one)

> - **Orbit Node (Where)** — Contextual relevance; domains the node relates to (many-to-many)

> - **Entanglement Node (How)** — Forward linkage; what the node leads to or affects (many-to-many)

> - **Tags** — Mood, energy, urgency, or keywords

> - **Status** — Phase in the flow: Inbox (New), Processing, Active, Dormant, Done, Archived, Deleted

> - **Energy Level** — Emotional/mental charge: Surge (Violet), High (Blue), Moderate (Green), Low (Orange), Blocked (Red)

> - **Reflective Score** — Auto-generated metric (0–9) based on detail richness, used for insight and filtering

> - **Orbit System (Group) – Optional** — Classical structure bridge: Projects, Tasks, Journals, etc.

> - **AI Tags** — Optional smart categorization

> - **Created/Edited Time & By** — For tracking and accountability

> - **QNO ID** — Auto-generated identifier (e.g., QNO-003)

> 

> > The page body is the Note.

> > 

> 

> Orbits represent gravitational centers in the QuNote system. A node may orbit a project, a topic, a mood, or a person. Orbit Nodes are contextual, while Orbit System groups reflect user-defined categories like PARA (Projects, Areas, Resources, Archives).

> 

> ### Flow tracks the node’s journey through:

> 

> - From intake to insight

> - From chaos to clarity

> - From idea to action

> 

> Nodes float until pulled into focus. Flow is nonlinear and supports ADHD time perception.
