> ---
> 
> ## What’s legit vs what’s *fishy*
> **✅ This type of business *can be real***  
> “Cash home buyer” companies do exist and buy houses *as-is* for cash. Many are legitimate investors, not outright scams. citeturn0search0
> 
> **⚠️ But they’re not charities / not there to give you fair market value**  
> Industry consensus (e.g., /r/RealEstate and many seller experiences) is that:
> - These companies generally **pay *well below* market value**  
> - They make money by reselling or wholesaling the property  
> - Some operators are legit; some are lowball or unreliable — it’s a mixed bag. citeturn0search8
> 
> **⚠️ ‘Hoosier Buyers’ name doesn’t show up as a *BBB accredited local business***  
> I couldn’t find a BBB profile or verified ratings for *offer.hoosierbuyers.com* directly. That’s a red flag if you’re expecting a *trusted*, local, established company.
> 
> Compare that to a **different verified company** — *Hoosier Trusted Home Buyers LLC* — which does have BBB accreditation and good ratings online. That’s a real entity you could evaluate. citeturn0search1turn0search6
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Bottom line: legit or nah?
> **Legit?**  
> *Maybe.* Just because it’s not an obvious scam doesn’t mean you should trust it blindly. There’s no strong evidence it’s a recognized, accredited operation.
> 
> **Safe?**  
> *Not by default.* You’re basically giving strangers your contact info for marketing and possibly an offer. There’s no strong trust signal like:
> - BBB accreditation
> - Verified customer reviews tied directly to this site
> - A clearly registered local business with a trackable history
> 
> **Worth engaging?**  
> Only if:
> - You *must* sell fast and understand you’ll likely get **well below market value**.
> - You verify who’s actually buying, check references, get proof of funds, and **compare multiple offers** before signing anything.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## If you were considering using it
> Do this *first* before giving an inch of personal info:
> - Google the company name + “scam / reviews / complaints”.  
> - Check local business registries (Indiana Secretary of State).  
> - Ask for **written proof of funds and a contract you can take to a real estate attorney/title company**.  
> - Compare with real offers from agents or vetted cash buyers (e.g., Clever Offers marketplace).
>
