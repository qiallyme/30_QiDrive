### Assistant
> Hey Q — good question. The % of cost spent on materials in a flooring business can vary quite a bit depending on material type, labor rates, waste, markup, job size, etc. But I can pull together a working range + what shifts it.
> 
> ---
> 
> ### Typical Material Cost Percentage
> 
> In many construction/trade businesses (flooring included), **materials often make up about 30-50%** of the total installed cost. The rest is labor, overhead, profit, etc.  
> 
> - If you use **high-end materials**, that % can creep above 50%.  
> - If it’s modest or basic materials + high labor or complex jobs, it might be closer to **25-35%**.  
> - For very premium jobs (exotic stone, custom tile, designer hardwoods) maybe materials are 50-60%+.
> 
> ---
> 
> ### What Influences the Material % (so you can tweak it)
> 
> To future-proof your business strategy: understand what moves the dial.
> 
> | Factor | Effect on Material % |
> |---|---|
> | Material quality / price point | More expensive materials → higher % share of cost. |
> | Labor cost / complexity | High labor (prep, removal, leveling, custom cuts) → lowers material % of total. |
> | Waste, scrap, over-ordering | More waste = effective material cost goes up, might increase % but cut into profit. |
> | Job size / scale | Larger jobs often get material cost efficiencies, reducing % somewhat. |
> | Geographic / local supply | If materials cost is high locally (transport, tariffs), % rises. If labor is really expensive locally, % shifts. |
> 
> ---
> 
> If you tell me your region & what kind of flooring you do (wood, vinyl, tile, etc.), I can give you a tighter rule of thumb.
