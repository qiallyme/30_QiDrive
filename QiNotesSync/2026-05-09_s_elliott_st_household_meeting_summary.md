# S Elliott St Household Meeting Summary

## Executive Summary

This meeting was a practical household operations check-in between Cody and mom. The conversation covered immediate groceries, short-term food planning, medical/mobility safety, bed and hygiene logistics, bill timing, telecom changes, caregiver income risk, and the larger goal of stabilizing the home so mom stays home, avoids preventable hospital returns, and the household can shift from reactive crisis mode into predictable monthly operations.

The strongest themes were:

1. **Keep mom home and medically stable.**
   Preventing another hospital visit is the core operational goal because hospitalization disrupts health, income, caregiving rhythm, transportation, and bills.

2. **Use limited money intentionally.**
   The immediate grocery budget was around **$55**, and the agreement was not to spend all of it automatically. The focus is essentials, meals that stretch, hydration, protein, and items that reduce friction.

3. **Build a one-month-ahead bill system.**
   Cody’s plan is to eventually put household bills into one account, pay ahead by a month, and run bills through autopay so nothing is missed or lost.

4. **Update the budget and remove dead services.**
   AT&T is gone. T-Mobile internet needs canceled or reduced. New internet/phone plan appears much cheaper and more useful.

5. **Mobility and bathroom access need immediate setup rules.**
   The bedside commode / porta-potty must be positioned close enough before mom attempts transfers. Prior placement created a safety issue and contributed to needing emergency help.

6. **Document symptoms and triggers.**
   Mom experienced dizziness after sitting upright after being sideways/in bed for a prolonged period. Possible causes discussed included morphine, not eating, blood sugar changes, pain, oxygen/breathing, and prolonged positioning. This needs to be tracked, not guessed.

## Decisions & Agreements

### 1. Grocery spending should be strategic, not automatic

The available grocery amount was treated as approximately **$55**, but Cody made clear that having $55 does **not** mean the household has to spend all $55.

**Agreement:**
- Prioritize what is actually needed.
- Focus on items that create multiple meals.
- Keep hydration and protein high-priority.
- Delay non-critical items when possible.

### 2. Immediate grocery priorities

Items discussed as needed or strongly preferred:

- Extra milk
- Macaroni salad
- Powerade, preferably red and sugar-free
- Bread
- Lunch meat, especially turkey
- Tuna
- Chips / snack chips
- Wipes
- Possibly cereal, depending on budget
- Protein sources that are affordable and easy

Items discussed but deprioritized or questioned:

- Ranch dressing: not needed right now
- Expensive protein drinks: likely too costly for the current budget
- Name-brand Cinnamon Toast Crunch: preferred by mom, but Cody flagged cost and sugar
- Walmart boneless wings: liked, but can wait and are not always available

### 3. Food strategy: protein + variety + low stomach friction

There was agreement that tuna, turkey, lunch meat, milk, and other practical foods help create more meals and variety.

Important nutritional logic:
- Tuna = protein
- Turkey/lunch meat = easy meals
- Some non-dairy protein options may help avoid stomach upset
- Drinks matter, especially during daytime

### 4. Mom should drink more during the day and less at night

Mom identified a pattern: too much drinking at night increases nighttime bathroom risk.

**Agreement:**
- Push more fluids earlier in the day.
- Reduce heavy nighttime drinking when possible.
- Keep hydration available but manage timing to reduce nighttime walking/fall risk.

### 5. The main household objective is keeping mom home

Mom stated the main objective clearly: keep her home, not sick, and getting back to better functioning.

Operational meaning:
- Avoid preventable hospital visits.
- Protect caregiver income.
- Protect household rhythm.
- Make the room safer.
- Improve transfer setup.
- Keep medical supplies and hygiene supplies ready.

### 6. Bedside commode / porta-potty placement must change

A prior issue happened because mom could not safely get from one location to the commode. This was serious enough that ambulance/emergency help became involved.

**Agreement:**
- The commode must be moved closer before mom needs it.
- This should be done proactively, not during urgency.
- Transfers and bathroom access are safety-critical.

### 7. Bed, floor, and hygiene reset is required when mom gets up

When mom is in the wheelchair or sitting up, Cody needs to use that window to reset the bed area.

Tasks discussed:
- Change bed linens.
- Clean urine from floor if present.
- Remove soiled items.
- Use clean blanket if main bedding is still washing/drying.
- Keep the bed dry overnight.
- Test new underwear/diaper setup to prevent bed wetting.

### 8. Mom should sit upright gradually before transferring

Mom became dizzy after sitting upright, likely because she had been sideways/in bed for a long period.

**Agreement:**
- Sit upright first.
- Wait until dizziness settles.
- Keep feet grounded.
- Use cool rag / airflow / breathing.
- Do not rush into wheelchair transfer while dizzy.
- Transfer only when stable enough.

### 9. Dizziness needs tracking before conclusions

Possible dizziness contributors discussed:

- Morphine
- Being sideways/in bed too long
- Not eating
- Blood sugar high/low
- Pain
- Oxygen/breathing
- General body stress after hospital/immobility

**Agreement:**
- Do not assume only one cause.
- Test and document patterns.
- Consider timing of food, morphine, oxygen, positioning, and movement.

### 10. Telecom/budget updates

Discussed telecom changes:

- AT&T is gone.
- T-Mobile internet needs to be canceled or at least the internet portion canceled.
- New internet plan first bill appears due next month.
- New plan discussed as around **$69 initially**, then **$40/month with autopay** for internet, with phone line details also mentioned in the broader context.

**Agreement:**
- Cancel/reduce T-Mobile.
- Update budget to remove old services.
- Fold new telecom costs into the household bill plan.

### 11. Bills need consolidated tracking

Bills specifically discussed:

- Affirm: **$19.25**, due around the **10th**, monthly
- Car insurance: discussed as around **$83.97**, due around the **13th**
- T-Mobile: cancel/review
- Water / CenterPoint / utility bills: need clarification based on bill timing and account ownership
- Laundry payment: still required
- Phone bill: changed/reduced due to plan changes

**Agreement:**
- Cody will add or update bill items.
- Some items need rechecked against the Google Sheet / budget tracker.
- Budget must remove canceled/obsolete services.
- Goal is one-month-ahead autopay.

### 12. Insurance quote should be reviewed

There was discussion that adding Cody and/or spouse information to insurance might reduce the rate, based on prior quote experience.

**Action direction:**
- Get a quote through Harkey / current insurance broker.
- Check whether household bundling or driver configuration reduces the premium.
- Do not assume current premium is optimized.

## Concerns / Risks

### Medical & care risks

1. **Dizziness during transfers**
   Mom became dizzy after sitting up. This is a fall risk and transfer risk.

2. **Bathroom distance**
   The commode being too far away is a real safety problem.

3. **Bed wetting / overnight hygiene**
   New underwear/diaper strategy needs testing to protect bed and reduce nighttime crisis.

4. **Oxygen awareness**
   Mom noted that being without oxygen affects her breathing and awareness. Oxygen setup should be treated as non-negotiable.

5. **Pain medication timing**
   Morphine may contribute to dizziness, but it is not proven from the conversation alone. Track it.

6. **Not eating before movement**
   Movement after not eating may worsen dizziness.

### Financial risks

1. **Hospitalization interrupts income**
   Mom noted that when she is hospitalized, Cody’s caregiver-related income stops or is delayed. This creates a household ripple effect.

2. **Too many bills held in memory**
   Several bills and dates were discussed verbally. This needs to be converted into a written tracker.

3. **Old services may keep draining money**
   T-Mobile/old telecom services must be canceled or verified.

4. **Credit risk from missed Affirm**
   Mom emphasized that Affirm should be paid to avoid damaging credit/access to future needs.

### Household operations risks

1. **Too much verbal switching**
   The meeting jumped between groceries, bills, care, insurance, mobility, symptoms, and telecom. Recording helps, but the system needs separate lists.

2. **Unclear responsibility map**
   Some tasks are Cody-owned, some mom-owned, some require calls, and some need support from others. These should be separated.

3. **Urgency can override setup**
   Bathroom, bed, and mobility tasks must happen before urgency hits.
