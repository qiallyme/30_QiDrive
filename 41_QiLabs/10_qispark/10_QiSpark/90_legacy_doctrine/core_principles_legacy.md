# 1.01 Core Principles

_The fundamental laws of awareness, presence, and system architecture._

---

## Statute

**I must observe before I act, be fully present to the moment and the work, and design all systems according to the QiEOS Founding Principles.**

Before making decisions, taking action, or forming judgments, I commit to:
1. **Observe** the full context without immediate reaction
2. **Reflect** on patterns, implications, and connections
3. **Document** observations when they inform future decisions
4. **Question** assumptions and first impressions
5. **Focus** on the task, person, or moment at hand
6. **Eliminate** distractions that pull attention away
7. **Embody** my values through action, not just intention
8. **Anchor** decisions in present reality, not hypothetical futures

## QiEOS Architectural Principles

All systems, structures, and processes must embody the QiEOS Protocol v3.0 (`legacy path: ../../../1.10_Protocol/QiEOS_Protocol_v3.0.md`) Founding Principles:

1. **Flat over nested** — Prefer shallow hierarchies over deep nesting
2. **Linked over duplicated** — Reference rather than copy content
3. **Documented over remembered** — Write it down, don't rely on memory
4. **Modular over massive** — Build small, composable units
5. **Truth over perfection** — Accuracy and honesty above polish

---

## Application

This principle applies to:
- Personal decisions and life choices
- Work priorities and project planning
- Relationships and interpersonal dynamics
- System design and automation (following QiEOS architectural principles)
- Learning and knowledge integration
- Work sessions and deep focus time
- Conversations and relationships
- Creative and technical work
- File and folder organization
- Documentation and knowledge management

---

## Exceptions

Strategic planning and future vision are exempted, but must be done with intentional presence during the planning session itself.
